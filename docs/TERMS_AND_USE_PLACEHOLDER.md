# Terms and Use Placeholder

This document is a plain-language placeholder for terms and acceptable use of
the **High-Complexity Contribution Tool** / **Instrument Complexiteit naar
Bijdrage**.

It is not final legal text. It is not a complete contract, privacy policy,
license, disclaimer, refund policy, employer pilot agreement, or professional
services agreement.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this placeholder is to make the intended and prohibited use
boundaries visible while the project is still a static Markdown/site prototype.

It should be reviewed and replaced before public launch, paid use, employer
pilots, live beta data collection, or professional/commercial distribution.

## 2. Current Prototype Use

Current state:

- Static prototype.
- Documentation and reflection materials.
- Markdown workbook and guide files.
- Local static website in `site/`.
- No live submission.
- No payment.
- No account system.
- No backend.
- No database.
- No external analytics provider.
- No stored beta request.

The current materials can be reviewed, copied, or used privately as draft
reflection and conversation support. They should not be presented as final
commercial, legal, clinical, HR, or occupational-health materials.

## 3. Acceptable Use

Acceptable use includes:

- Individual reflection by a gifted, hoogbegaafde, or high-complexity adult.
- Voluntary coaching, advisory, career, or facilitation use.
- Responsible manager conversation focused on role/context conditions.
- HR or people-development support where participation is voluntary and the
  adult controls what personal material is shared.
- Bounded pilot use where scope, privacy, documentation, and stop conditions are
  clear.
- Product review, editorial review, or private beta feedback about clarity,
  usefulness, safety, boundaries, packaging, or missing guidance.

Acceptable use should focus on:

- Concrete work examples.
- Person-role-context fit.
- Role levers and legitimate constraints.
- One bounded 30-day experiment.
- Human review and participant agency.

## 4. Prohibited Use

Do not use the tool for:

- Hiring.
- Promotion.
- Firing.
- Selection.
- Ranking.
- Succession decisions.
- High-potential decisions.
- Surveillance.
- Mandatory disclosure.
- Diagnosis.
- Psychological screening.
- Medical decision-making.
- Clinical advice or treatment planning.
- Occupational-health determinations.
- Disability determinations.
- Formal accommodation decisions.
- Legal decisions.
- Disciplinary process.
- Performance rating.
- Support-entitlement decisions.
- Employer-only evaluation without voluntary adult participation.
- Automated scoring, dashboards, comparison, or interpretation of completed
  personal responses.

If a use requires a score, eligibility decision, diagnosis, legal conclusion,
clinical conclusion, employment decision, or ranking, it is outside scope.

## 5. User Responsibilities

Users are responsible for using judgment and staying within scope.

Users should:

- Treat the tool as structured reflection and conversation support.
- Share selectively.
- Keep private workbook responses private unless sharing is safe, useful, and
  voluntary.
- Avoid entering sensitive data in non-secure channels.
- Avoid sending completed scans, health details, HR records, conflict
  narratives, or third-party personal information through prototype forms,
  email, or feedback channels unless a reviewed process exists.
- Not represent the tool as validated, diagnostic, clinical, legal,
  occupational-health, or employment-decision support.
- Not use outputs as employment decisions.
- Pause use when the situation is unsafe, adversarial, disciplinary,
  legally sensitive, health-related, or outside ordinary role-design scope.

## 6. Employer and Facilitator Responsibilities

Employers, managers, HR partners, coaches, advisors, and facilitators have
additional responsibilities because the material can involve power dynamics and
sensitive work-context information.

They should:

- Keep participation voluntary.
- State the purpose and scope before use.
- Ensure there are no adverse consequences from non-participation.
- Preserve adult control over what personal material is shared.
- Avoid requesting diagnoses, IQ scores, giftedness proof, health details,
  conflict narratives, or completed private workbook content.
- Avoid storing workbook details in HR files.
- Use outputs as role-design hypotheses only.
- Document only agreed role-design actions, review evidence, stop conditions,
  and operational commitments when documentation is needed.
- Keep the conversation separate from hiring, promotion, performance,
  discipline, firing, ranking, support entitlement, and formal HR decisions.
- Use facilitation or another appropriate professional route when conflict,
  health, legal, disability, accommodation, or occupational-health issues are
  central.

## 7. Limitations

The current tool is a prototype.

It is:

- Not validated.
- Not normed.
- Not diagnostic.
- Not a psychometric instrument.
- Not clinical advice.
- Not legal advice.
- Not HR compliance advice.
- Not occupational-health advice.
- Not medical advice.
- Not a guarantee of any workplace, health, legal, commercial, or performance
  outcome.

The tool may help structure a useful conversation, but it cannot guarantee
agreement, safety, role redesign, improved performance, reduced burnout,
reduced conflict, retention, promotion, accommodation, or commercial results.
