# Marketing and Launch Messaging

This document is the source copy and messaging guide for beta and later launch
materials for the **High-Complexity Contribution Tool** / **Instrument
Complexiteit naar Bijdrage**.

It is copy source only. It does not implement a live beta form, email capture,
accounts, payments, analytics providers, backend APIs, deployment, or data
collection.

## 1. Purpose

This document gives the project a single place to hold positioning, claims,
audience language, website copy, launch copy, outreach copy, social copy, and
commercial messaging boundaries.

Use it for:

- Private beta invitations.
- Website copy drafts.
- LinkedIn and network launch copy.
- Coach, facilitator, manager, HR, and employer outreach.
- Claim review before public materials are published.
- Consistent wording across future README, site, FAQ, and sales materials.

All claims must stay within the current product reality:

- The product is a Markdown-first workbook and static-site prototype.
- The current site beta area has no input fields and does not submit, store,
  email, or send data.
- There are no accounts, payments, backend APIs, external analytics providers,
  email capture integrations, or live delivery systems.
- The beta is manual and private.
- The tool is not validated as a commercial instrument.
- The tool is non-diagnostic, non-selection, non-ranking, and not for
  performance management.
- Employer use must remain voluntary, bounded, role/context oriented, and
  outside HR decision-making.

Do not use this document to create stronger claims than the artifacts support.
Where wording feels attractive but would imply validation, outcome guarantees,
diagnosis, ranking, employee assessment, health impact, or public readiness,
choose the safer version.

## 5. Long Pitch

### Adult-Facing Long Pitch

Some work problems are hard to explain because they are not only about task
volume, motivation, communication, or performance. The role may not use enough
complexity. The decision space may be too narrow. Learning may have stopped
too quickly. Quality, ethics, logic, or risk concerns may have no serious
conversation route. The person may be carrying hidden translation work,
meeting load, or overdrive that is not visible from the outside.

The High-Complexity Contribution Tool helps you sort those signals privately
before you decide what to say at work. It gives you prompts for concrete work
examples, disclosure boundaries, role-fit dimensions, and one practical
role-design request.

The goal is not to prove giftedness, explain your whole identity, or produce a
score. The goal is to decide what is useful and safe to share, then turn one
fit hypothesis into a bounded 30-day experiment with clear evidence and stop
conditions.

The current version is an early Markdown workbook and beta package. It is not
validated, not diagnostic, and not a live self-serve product. Use it as
structured preparation, ideally with a coach or facilitator when the situation
is sensitive.

### Coach or Facilitator Long Pitch

Coaches, advisors, HR partners, and facilitators often meet adults whose work
friction is too complex for generic career advice but too sensitive for casual
giftedness framing. The client may be underused, overextended, bored, intense,
values-driven, quality-sensitive, fast-learning, or close to conflict or exit.
The manager may see behavior and friction but not the role conditions behind
them.

The High-Complexity Contribution Tool provides a structured, non-diagnostic
workflow for that conversation. It separates private reflection from shared
work language, translates patterns into role/context hypotheses, and keeps the
output practical: one bounded 30-day work experiment or a responsible decision
to pause.

The tool is not an assessment, certification route, diagnosis, score, or
performance instrument. Its value is in safer structure, clearer prompts,
privacy boundaries, role levers, and reviewable next steps.

The private beta should help test whether the materials are usable in real
sessions without author explanation, what scripts or examples are missing, and
whether a professional pack is commercially credible.

### Employer-Facing Long Pitch

In some roles, high complexity shows up as fast mastery, quality concerns,
systems thinking, strong autonomy needs, repeated translation work, values
friction, boredom, overdrive, or unusual intensity. These signals are easy to
misread as attitude, fragility, resistance, arrogance, or performance trouble.

The High-Complexity Contribution Tool helps managers and HR partners look at
the role and context before judging the person. It asks practical questions:
is the work complex enough, is the decision space clear, is learning still
present, is there a route for quality dialogue, is sparring available, and is
the contribution sustainable?

The intended output is one small role-design experiment, not a conclusion about
the employee. Employer use must be voluntary, bounded, and focused on work
conditions. The tool must not be used for hiring, selection, promotion,
performance rating, ranking, discipline, support entitlement, diagnosis, or HR
decision-making.

The current product is still a prototype and manual beta package. Any employer
pilot would need written scope, adult control over private material, clear
documentation boundaries, facilitation where needed, and no storage of private
workbook content in HR files.

### General Website Long Pitch

The High-Complexity Contribution Tool is a workbook-first role-design package
for adults and work contexts that need better language for complex
contribution.

It helps turn vague tension around underuse, overload, autonomy, learning,
quality, sparring, translation, intensity, energy, and boundaries into a
structured conversation and one bounded 30-day experiment.

The product is built for practical use by adults, coaches, managers, HR
partners, and facilitators. It is non-diagnostic, non-scoring, and not for HR
ranking or employment decisions. The adult controls what private material is
shared. Employer-side use stays focused on role and context conditions.

The current version is a Markdown/static-site prototype in private beta
preparation. There is no live form submission, account system, payment flow,
external analytics provider, or validated commercial assessment. The beta is
for learning whether the materials are clear, useful, safe enough to use, and
commercially credible.

### FAQ

#### Is this a giftedness test?

No. It does not prove giftedness, measure IQ, certify a label, or diagnose
anyone. Giftedness is one possible audience language, not the product output.

#### Is it a personality test?

No. It is a role-design workbook and conversation guide. It does not produce a
type, score, profile, ranking, or assessment result.

#### What does the tool produce?

The intended output is a concrete role-design hypothesis and one bounded
30-day experiment, or a responsible decision to pause, narrow, or seek another
support route.

- "Informed by existing thinking about giftedness, high-complexity work,
  role fit, adult agency, and sustainable contribution."
- "Currently being tested for clarity, safety, usefulness, and commercial fit."

Always pair with:

- "Not validated as a commercial assessment."
- "Not diagnostic."
- "Not a score, ranking, or outcome predictor."
- "Beta learning is product learning, not proof of efficacy."

### How to Discuss Lack of Validation Honestly

Say:

> The tool is not validated as a commercial assessment or outcome predictor.
> The private beta is intended to test whether the materials are clear, useful,
> safe enough to use, and commercially credible within strict boundaries.

Do not soften this with "but". The limitation is part of the trust message.

### How to Cite Internal Research Artifacts Without Overclaiming

Use internal artifacts as support for product design decisions:

- PRD: product scope, use cases, out-of-scope boundaries.
- Workbook and guides: current workflow and artifact support.
- Responsible-use guide: allowed and disallowed use.
- Beta plan: manual beta waves and feedback process.
- Analytics plan: privacy-first learning and event limits.
- Trust review: launch risks and current readiness.
- Monetization plan: pricing hypotheses and commercial boundaries.

Do not cite internal artifacts as proof that the tool works, reduces churn,
improves performance, prevents burnout, diagnoses giftedness, or is validated.

### How to Avoid Leaning on Lapré or Any Single Source Too Heavily

- Use no single source as the authority for the product claim.
- If research or conceptual sources are cited later, present them as context,
  not validation.
- Balance giftedness sources with work-design, coaching, adult learning,
  organizational behavior, privacy, ethics, and role-design reasoning.
- Prefer "informed by" over "based on".
- Avoid source-name dropping in short marketing copy unless the source is
  directly relevant and accurately represented.
- Do not imply one researcher, model, or paper proves the product.

### How to Talk About Beta Learning

Say:

- "We are testing clarity."
- "We are testing whether the workflow produces concrete next steps."
- "We are testing whether boundaries are understandable."
- "We are testing whether professionals can use the materials responsibly."
- "We are testing whether a paid package is credible."

Do not say:

- "We are validating outcomes."
- "We are proving the model."
- "We are measuring impact."
- "We are collecting performance data."
- "We are building an evidence base from user cases."

## 19. Commercial Messaging Boundaries

### What Can Be Sold Now

Nothing is implemented for live self-serve sale in the current repository.
There is no checkout, payment provider, account system, license delivery,
download fulfillment, public terms, final privacy policy, or live support
route.

What can be discussed now:

- Manual private beta review.
- Product feedback conversations.
- Future professional-pack interest.
- Future workshop or pilot interest.
- Pricing hypotheses.
- Commercial discovery.

### What Should Only Be Piloted

- Professional pack with coaches, advisors, HR partners, or facilitators.
- Founder-led workshop or guided session.
- Bounded employer pilot.
- Individual paid workbook, if self-guided use proves safe enough.

Each pilot needs clear scope, responsible-use acceptance, data boundaries,
support boundaries, and no completed-scan collection by the product owner.

### What Should Not Be Sold Yet

- Broad employer rollout.
- Certification.
- Train-the-trainer program.
- Validated assessment.
- Talent identification.
- High-potential program.
- Employee dashboard.
- Automated interpretation.
- Account-based completion tracking.
- Analytics on personal workbook responses.
- Public self-serve checkout.

### What Requires Legal/Privacy Review First

- Any live beta form or mailing list.
- Any payment, invoice, refund, tax, or license workflow.
- Any public terms of use.
- Any privacy policy or data processing arrangement.
- Any support or security contact process.
- Any employer pilot agreement.
- Any external analytics provider.
- Any collection, retention, deletion, or access process for beta contacts or
  feedback.

### What Requires Validation First

- Claims of efficacy.
- Claims of improved retention, engagement, productivity, performance, health,
  burnout, conflict, absenteeism, or manager behavior.
- Claims that the tool measures, predicts, identifies, or classifies anything.
- Certification or professional authority claims.
- Broad employer standardization.
- Benchmarking or comparison across employees, teams, or organizations.
