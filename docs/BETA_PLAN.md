# Private Beta Operating Plan

This plan defines how to run a small private beta for the
**High-Complexity Contribution Tool** / **Instrument Complexiteit naar
Bijdrage** while the product is still a Markdown/static-site prototype.

It does not implement accounts, authentication, payments, backend APIs,
databases, analytics providers, cookies, tracking scripts, live forms, or
third-party services. It assumes beta interest, delivery, support, and feedback
are handled manually.

## 1. Purpose

The private beta exists to learn whether the current workbook-first package is
clear, useful, safe enough to use, and commercially plausible before broader
launch work starts.

The beta should produce product learning about:

- Problem recognition.
- Usability of the workbook and supporting guides.
- Trust and emotional safety.
- Responsible-use boundary clarity.
- Privacy and disclosure handling.
- Manager and employer friendliness.
- Facilitation needs.
- Commercial packaging and pricing interest.

The beta is not for:

- Diagnosis.
- IQ testing or proving giftedness.
- Hiring, selection, promotion, or employee ranking.
- Performance evaluation or disciplinary framing.
- Clinical, legal, occupational-health, disability, or HR case advice.
- Employer decision-making about a person.

The expected beta output is product learning and, where appropriate, one
bounded 30-day role-design experiment. It is not a conclusion about a person.

## 2. Beta Scope

Included in the beta:

- Manual review of a small number of interested participants.
- Manual delivery of draft Markdown materials.
- Private individual reflection by adults.
- Coach, advisor, or facilitator review and small facilitated use.
- Manager/HR role-context review only where participation is voluntary and
  bounded.
- Structured feedback about clarity, safety, usefulness, missing artifacts,
  trust, and commercial willingness.
- Privacy-first notes that avoid completed workbook content.

Excluded from the beta:

- Live beta form submission.
- Email capture provider or mailing-list automation.
- Accounts, login, dashboard, portal, or entitlement system.
- Payment, checkout, subscription, invoice automation, or license delivery.
- Backend storage, database, file upload, or analytics provider.
- Collection of completed scans, HR case details, health details, conflict
  narratives, employee lists, or identifiable workplace stories.
- Employer rollout, mandatory program, selection use, performance use, or
  employee comparison.

Participants may use these artifacts:

- `docs/WORKBOOK_v1.md`
- `docs/INTERPRETATION_GUIDE_v1.md`
- `docs/MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md`
- `docs/USER_GUIDE.md`
- `docs/V1_PACKAGE_INDEX.md`
- `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md`
- `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`
- `docs/PRIVACY_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/SECURITY.md`
- `docs/TERMS_AND_USE_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- The legacy v0.1 adult, employer, and joint canvas files if useful for
  comparison.

Static, placeholder, or manual parts:

- The site beta form is a placeholder only. It stores nothing, submits
  nothing, and sends nothing.
- Analytics event names are local/no-op only. No provider is connected.
- Privacy, security, and terms materials are draft placeholders.
- Beta delivery is manual.
- Feedback capture is manual.
- Pricing is a hypothesis, not a live offer.

Do not treat the beta as production-ready. It is not ready for public launch,
public data collection, paid checkout, employer rollout, or self-serve use at
scale.

## 4. Excluded Beta Participants and Use Cases

Decline or redirect requests involving:

- Hiring.
- Screening.
- Candidate comparison.
- Promotion.
- Succession.
- High-potential ranking.
- Performance review.
- Employee ranking.
- Capability judgment.
- Support-entitlement decisions.
- Diagnosis or clinical assessment.
- IQ testing or proof of giftedness.
- Mandatory employer program.
- Employer-only evaluation without voluntary adult participation.
- Active unsafe conflict context.
- Disciplinary, grievance, firing, legal, health, disability,
  occupational-health, or formal accommodation situations.
- Situations requiring legal, clinical, occupational-health, or HR case advice.
- Requests for scoring, dashboards, automated interpretation, certification, or
  validated assessment claims.

When a request is out of scope, keep the response short: explain that the tool
is only for voluntary role-design reflection and conversation support, then
decline the beta use.

## 5. Beta Waves

| Wave | Goal | Participant type | Entry criteria | What to test | Exit criteria | Stop conditions |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wave 0: internal review | Confirm the package is safe enough to show to trusted outsiders. | Maintainer, trusted reviewer, or content reviewer. | All required draft docs exist; site copy says form does not submit; no live data collection. | Boundaries, broken links, confusing claims, missing onboarding text, tracker fields. | No obvious live-form, privacy, diagnosis, selection, or launch-readiness confusion remains. | Any copy implies diagnosis, validation, live intake, payment, analytics, or employer decision support. |
| Wave 1: 3 to 5 trusted individual users | Test individual recognition, clarity, privacy, and usefulness. | Trusted adults and possibly one professional reviewer. | Participant accepts beta disclosure and agrees not to send sensitive content. | Problem framing, workbook clarity, disclosure boundaries, emotional safety, concrete next step. | At least 3 structured feedback responses; no unmanageable misuse or privacy issue. | Users seek diagnosis/proof, feel pushed to disclose, or cannot understand the boundaries. |

| Wave 3: 1 to 2 very bounded employer/manager conversations | Test employer-side language and governance under tight scope. | Manager/HR participant with voluntary adult involvement and preferably facilitation. | Written scope, voluntary participation, no adverse consequence, no HR decision use, documentation boundary. | Manager guide, role/context framing, adult agency, HR-file limits, stop conditions, review evidence. | Employer accepts constraints and a conversation can end in role-design action or responsible pause. | Any hiring, performance, ranking, discipline, support-entitlement, mandatory, or unsafe conflict pressure appears. |

Do not advance waves only because there is interest. Advance when the previous
wave shows clearer use, manageable risk, and concrete product learning.

## 6. Intake Workflow

Interest should be expressed manually through a private contact route until a
reviewed beta intake route exists. The static site form must remain a
placeholder until privacy, consent, retention, deletion, and support handling
are ready.

Request only information needed to decide fit and follow up:

- Name or professional contact name.
- Email or agreed private contact route.
- Participant type.
- Intended use category.
- Whether facilitation is involved.
- Country or timezone only if needed for scheduling.
- Confirmation that the person accepts the beta boundaries.
- Confirmation that they will not send completed scans or sensitive details.

Do not request:

- Completed workbook responses.
- Diagnosis, IQ, giftedness proof, health, burnout, disability, treatment, or
  occupational-health information.
- Legal, grievance, disciplinary, performance, promotion, or HR case details.
- Full conflict narratives.
- Employee, manager, colleague, client, candidate, or team names.
- Files, screenshots, exports, or records from workplace systems.

Suggested intake fields:

| Field | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Participant ID | Manual code | Use `BETA-001`, `BETA-002`, etc. |
| Name/contact | Short text | Store only in the contact record. |
| Email/contact route | Short text | Used for beta follow-up only. |
| Participant type | Category | Adult, coach/advisor/facilitator, manager/HR, employer pilot candidate, reviewer. |
| Intended use category | Category | Private reflection, manager conversation preparation, facilitated use, employer pilot review, commercial evaluation. |
| Wave candidate | Category | Wave 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4. |
| Boundary acknowledgement | Yes/no | Non-diagnostic, non-selection, no completed scans, no sensitive content. |
| Fit decision | Category | Accept, clarify, waitlist, decline, refer elsewhere. |
| Minimal notes | Short text | Product-learning notes only, no sensitive content. |

Fit decision rules:

- Accept when the use is voluntary, role/context oriented, privacy-aware, and
  appropriate for the current wave.
- Ask for non-sensitive clarification when the use is vague but could fit.
- Waitlist when the participant type is useful but too advanced for the current
  wave.
- Decline when the request seeks diagnosis, proof, selection, performance,
  ranking, mandatory employer use, legal/clinical/occupational-health advice,
  or unsafe conflict handling.

For unsuitable requests, do not debate details. State the boundary, decline the
beta use, and avoid collecting more case information.

Record minimal beta status without sensitive content:

- Participant code.
- Type.
- Wave.
- Fit decision.
- Docs sent.
- Acknowledgement status.
- Feedback status.
- Commercial interest category.
- Follow-up status.

Keep contact records separate from product-learning notes.

## 7. Onboarding Workflow

Before sending beta materials, send:

- `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md`
- `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`
- `docs/PRIVACY_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/SECURITY.md`
- `docs/TERMS_AND_USE_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/BETA_PLAN.md` if the participant is a coach, facilitator, manager, HR
  partner, employer pilot candidate, or paid-pilot prospect.

Explain the boundaries in plain language:

- This is an early workbook prototype.
- It is not a diagnosis, assessment, score, proof, legal/clinical/HR decision
  tool, or performance instrument.
- Completed scans should not be sent to the maintainer.
- Feedback should be about the product, not sensitive personal facts.
- The adult controls what private material is shared.
- Employer-side use must stay focused on role/context conditions.

Ask for acknowledgement before use:

- "I understand this is a non-diagnostic prototype."
- "I will not use it for hiring, promotion, performance, ranking, diagnosis, or
  support-entitlement decisions."
- "I will not send completed workbook responses or sensitive personal, health,
  conflict, HR, legal, or third-party information."
- "I understand feedback should focus on clarity, usefulness, safety,
  boundaries, missing guidance, and commercial fit."

Recommend safe use:

- Start with private reading.
- Complete only the sections needed for the current question.
- Use concrete work examples.
- Create a manager-safe summary instead of sharing the full private workbook.
- Pause if the situation is unsafe, adversarial, disciplinary, legal,
  health-related, or too sensitive.
- Use facilitation when power dynamics or conflict risk are material.

For manager-supported use, prepare:

- A written purpose.
- Voluntary participation statement.
- A no-adverse-consequence statement.
- A list of what will not be requested or stored.
- A documentation boundary.
- A review date and stop conditions.
- A facilitator or neutral process owner if needed.

## 8. Participant Instructions

### Individual User

- Use the workbook privately first.
- Do not send completed scans to the maintainer as feedback.
- Choose one or two concrete work examples, not a full personal history.
- Mark what stays private before any manager conversation.
- Draft one practical role-design request.
- Test one bounded 30-day experiment only if the context is safe enough.
- Stop or seek other support if the real issue is health, legal,
  occupational-health, disciplinary, or unsafe conflict.

### Coach or Facilitator

- Present the tool as structured reflection and role-design support.
- Do not call it a validated assessment, diagnosis, profile, score, or
  certification route.
- Protect the adult's right not to share private material.
- Translate patterns into work examples and role levers.
- Keep notes limited to the agreed service purpose.
- Give feedback on where scripts, examples, handouts, or safeguards are
  missing.
- Do not send client workbook content as beta feedback.

### Manager or HR Participant

- Use the material to examine role/context conditions, not the person's worth
  or capability.
- Do not request private workbook content.
- Do not store completed scans in HR files.
- Do not use the tool for hiring, promotion, performance, ranking,
  discipline, support entitlement, diagnosis, or formal accommodation
  decisions.
- Document only agreed role-design actions, review evidence, stop conditions,
  and operational commitments.
- Use facilitation when the power dynamic or conflict level makes voluntary
  participation uncertain.

### Employer Pilot Sponsor

- Treat employer use as a scoped pilot, not a rollout.
- State purpose, participant count, time box, outputs, and stop conditions in
  writing.
- Confirm participation is voluntary and non-participation has no adverse
  consequence.
- Accept that no employee analytics, comparison, scoring, diagnosis, ranking,
  or HR decision use is allowed.
- Keep adult private material out of employer records.
- Appoint a facilitator or process owner.
- Review only product learning, role/context actions, and governance issues.

## 9. Feedback Workflow

Request feedback at four points:

- After onboarding: did the boundaries and purpose make sense?
- After first review of materials: what was clear, confusing, missing, or too
  sensitive?
- After use or attempted use: did the material produce a useful next step,
  experiment, or responsible pause?
- After 30 to 45 days: what learning, follow-up, commercial interest, or
  safety issue appeared?

How to request feedback:

- Use a short email or call.
- Ask category questions first.
- Keep free text optional and clearly marked: no sensitive details.
- Ask about product wording, workflow, trust, safety, and usefulness.
- Ask for commercial interest without pressuring purchase.

What not to ask:

- "Send your completed workbook."
- "Describe the full conflict."
- "What diagnosis or IQ score is involved?"
- "Who is the manager or employee?"
- "What performance, legal, HR, health, or accommodation issue is this?"

Separate tool feedback from sensitive content:

- Ask whether a section was useful, not what the participant wrote in it.
- Ask whether a conversation produced a category of outcome, not the private
  content of that outcome.
- Record themes such as "manager guide too long" or "privacy boundary clear".
- Do not copy personal stories into product notes.

Quotes and testimonials:

- Do not use beta feedback publicly by default.
- Ask separate, explicit permission for any quote.
- Confirm exact wording before use.
- Offer anonymous or generic attribution.
- Do not publish quotes that reveal sensitive personal, health, conflict, HR,
  employer, or third-party information.

Anonymization:

- Use participant IDs in learning notes.
- Remove names, organizations, job titles, locations, dates, and team details
  unless they are necessary and permission exists.
- Generalize small or recognizable contexts.
- Prefer aggregate learning over stories.

Weekly feedback summary:

- Count accepted, declined, waiting, and unsuitable beta requests.
- Count feedback by participant type and wave.
- Summarize top clarity issues.
- Summarize top safety or misuse concerns.
- Summarize missing artifacts.
- Summarize experiment outcomes or responsible pauses.
- Summarize commercial interest and pricing reactions.
- Decide whether the next change belongs in immediate artifact revision,
  `docs/OPEN_ITEMS.md`, or a future release note.

## 10. Feedback Question Bank

| Category | Questions |
|---|---|
| Problem recognition | What phrase made the work problem recognizable? What felt irrelevant, elitist, diagnostic, or too abstract? |
| Clarity | Which section was easiest to understand? Where did you need extra explanation? Which instruction should be shorter or more concrete? |
| Emotional safety | Did any prompt create pressure to disclose too much? Did the boundaries help you decide what stays private? What would make use safer? |
| Practical usefulness | Could you name one fit dimension, one work example, and one next step? Could you draft a 30-day experiment or decide to pause? |
| Employer friendliness | Would the manager-facing language work in a real workplace? Which terms would trigger HR, performance, or diagnosis concerns? |
| Role-design usefulness | Which role levers felt actionable: complexity lane, decision zone, focus time, learning sprint, quality dialogue, sparring route, translation support, or workload protection? |
| Trust and evidence | What made the tool feel trustworthy? What evidence, example, review, or caveat was missing? Which claim would you distrust? |
| Commercial willingness | Which offer would you consider: individual workbook, professional pack, workshop, facilitated session, or employer pilot? What would need to be included? |
| Pricing sensitivity | At EUR 39-79, what would an individual workbook need to include? At EUR 249-499, what would make a professional pack credible? What would make pricing feel unsupported? |
| Missing artifacts | What would you need next: facilitator guide, synthetic example, tracker, review template, sample report, checklist, FAQ, PDF, DOCX, or bilingual version? |
| Language friction | Which words worked? Which words caused friction, such as gifted, hoogbegaafd, high-complexity, intensity, autonomy, performance, talent, or assessment? |

Use these questions selectively. Do not turn feedback into a long survey unless
the participant has agreed to a structured interview.

## 11. Success Criteria

These are beta decision criteria, not validation claims.

| Area | Success signal |
|---|---|
| Problem framing validated | Qualified participants recognize the role-fit problem without needing diagnosis, IQ, or proof language. |
| Workbook useful enough | Adults can use the workbook privately, choose what stays private, and draft a concrete role-design request or decide to pause. |
| Manager conversation guide useful enough | Managers, HR partners, or facilitators can keep the conversation focused on role/context conditions and one bounded experiment. |
| Responsible-use boundaries understood | Participants can explain that the tool is not diagnostic, not for selection, not for performance review, and not for employer decision-making. |
| Commercial package interest | At least some qualified professionals or buyers ask for a professional pack, workshop, facilitated use, or pilot without asking for assessment claims. |
| Pricing hypothesis worth testing | Price reactions are specific enough to test one manual offer, not only general encouragement. |
| Employer pilot readiness | A very bounded employer candidate accepts voluntary use, documentation limits, no HR-decision use, facilitation, and stop conditions. |
| Public launch not yet ready | Open launch blockers remain visible: final privacy/terms/security review, contact route, link/accessibility QA, support process, and vendor review if any services are added. |

The beta is successful only if usefulness and boundary understanding develop
together.

## 12. Failure Signals and Pivot Triggers

| Signal | What it may mean | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Framing does not resonate | The language is too niche, too abstract, or too identity-heavy. | Test role-fit, sustainable contribution, learning, autonomy, and quality-language alternatives. |
| Tool feels too abstract | Prompts do not translate into concrete work examples. | Add synthetic examples, role levers, checklists, and sample outputs. |
| Tool feels too sensitive | Adults fear disclosure or employer storage. | Strengthen private-use guidance, manager-safe summaries, and facilitation routes. |
| Users want diagnosis | The positioning attracts proof/assessment expectations. | Tighten qualification, FAQ, and refusal language; avoid score/result wording. |
| Employers want selection use | Employer interest is misaligned. | Decline those requests and keep employer access manual. |
| Coaches overclaim | Professional use may drift into assessment or certification. | Clarify license/copy, add facilitator boundaries, and delay professional scaling. |
| Too much facilitation required | Self-guided use may not be ready or the material is too hard to apply. | Narrow to facilitated/professional pack first. |
| Pricing feels unsupported | The package is not yet concrete or credible enough to sell. | Add examples, tracker, facilitator materials, and clearer deliverables before payment. |
| Evidence base feels weak or unbalanced | Claims may sound stronger than current support. | Keep claims modest and add evidence review before public claims. |
| Role-design outputs are not concrete enough | The workflow is not delivering the intended product value. | Strengthen experiment templates, manager actions, review evidence, and stop conditions. |

Stop a wave if failure signals create privacy risk, misuse risk, or pressure on
adults to disclose personal material.

## 13. Support and Issue Process

Manual support route:

- Use the private contact channel already used for the beta until a monitored
  public contact route exists.
- Do not use the static site form for support.
- Do not ask participants to send completed workbook content.
- Keep issue notes product-focused and redacted.

Participants should report:

- Broken links or unclear docs.
- Confusing instructions.
- Missing artifacts.
- Wording that feels diagnostic, unsafe, overclaiming, or employer-risky.
- Form/site behavior that appears to submit or store data.
- Any pressure to use the tool for diagnosis, selection, performance, ranking,
  or mandatory disclosure.
- Any accidental sensitive disclosure so it can be deleted or redacted.

Participants should not send:

- Completed scans.
- Health, diagnosis, burnout, disability, treatment, or occupational-health
  details.
- Legal, grievance, disciplinary, performance, promotion, or HR case details.
- Names of employees, managers, clients, colleagues, candidates, or teams.
- Employer records, screenshots, exports, or confidential files.

Bug or issue template:

```text
Participant ID:
Date:
Artifact or site section:
Issue type: broken link / unclear wording / missing guidance / site behavior / other
Short description:
Steps to reproduce, if relevant:
Suggested fix, if any:
Sensitive content included? no / yes, please delete or redact
```

Safety or misuse concern template:

```text
Participant ID:
Date:
Concern category: diagnosis / hiring / performance / ranking / pressure to disclose / unsafe conflict / privacy / overclaim / other
Where it appeared:
Short product-focused description, without personal case details:
Immediate action needed? pause / decline / revise wording / delete sensitive note / other
Follow-up status:
```

Response expectations:

- Acknowledge safety or privacy concerns before ordinary product feedback.
- Delete or redact accidental sensitive disclosures where practical.
- Decline out-of-scope uses without collecting more detail.
- Log issue categories weekly.
- Do not promise clinical, legal, occupational-health, HR, privacy, or security
  advice.

## 16. Changelog and Learning Log

Record beta learning weekly in a short internal note or spreadsheet tab.

Minimum weekly learning log:

- Date range.
- Wave.
- Participants invited, onboarded, declined, and waiting.
- Main clarity issue.
- Main safety or misuse issue.
- Main missing artifact.
- Experiment outcome categories.
- Commercial signal.
- Pricing signal.
- Change decision.
- Owner and follow-up date.

Decide where each change belongs:

- Use immediate artifact revision for wording that creates safety, privacy,
  diagnosis, selection, or performance-management risk.
- Use `docs/OPEN_ITEMS.md` for useful work that is not part of the current
  step or cannot be handled immediately.
- Use a future release note for resolved changes that affect participant-facing
  materials, package scope, pricing, delivery, or responsible-use guidance.
- Use no change when feedback is isolated, out of scope, or would increase
  privacy/misuse risk.

Suggested changelog categories:

- Boundary language.
- Privacy/disclosure.
- Workbook clarity.
- Manager/HR guidance.
- Facilitator guidance.
- Role-design experiment template.
- Examples and sample outputs.
- Pricing/package.
- Site wording.
- Support/process.
- Legal/privacy/security placeholder.
- Employer pilot governance.

Do not include sensitive participant stories in changelog entries.

## 17. Exit Criteria

| Transition | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Move from internal review to beta | Required docs are linked; site form clearly says it does not submit; no live data collection exists; intake and feedback templates exist; stop conditions are written. |
| Move from individual beta to facilitated beta | Wave 1 users recognize the problem, understand boundaries, and give product feedback without unmanageable sensitive disclosures. |
| Move from facilitated beta to employer pilot | Facilitators can use the materials responsibly; manager language is concrete; privacy boundaries hold; a written employer pilot scope and stop process are ready. |
| Move from free beta to paid pilot | A specific paid package has credible demand; delivery, support, terms, privacy, refund/license scope, and responsible-use acceptance can be handled manually. |
| Move from private beta to public launch | Final privacy, terms, security/contact, support, link/accessibility QA, public claims review, and any vendor/data-flow review are complete; misuse and disclosure risks are manageable. |

Do not move to public launch while the current form is still only a placeholder
unless the site makes that limitation unmistakable.

## 18. Beta Launch Checklist

Before inviting real beta participants, confirm:

- [ ] Artifact readiness: core workbook, interpretation guide, manager guide,
  user guide, beta disclosure, responsible-use guide, privacy placeholder,
  security posture, terms placeholder, and beta plan are available.
- [ ] Site wording: beta area clearly says the form does not submit, store, or
  send anything.
- [ ] Contact route: manual beta contact route is known to invited
  participants.
- [ ] Disclosure and consent: beta acknowledgement wording is ready.
- [ ] Privacy placeholder: current data posture and no-sensitive-content rules
  are visible.
- [ ] Terms placeholder: acceptable and prohibited uses are visible.
- [ ] Responsible-use guide: adult, coach, manager, HR, and employer rules are
  visible.
- [ ] Manual tracker: participant status can be recorded without sensitive
  content.
- [ ] Feedback questions: product-focused question bank is ready.
- [ ] Support process: issue and misuse concern templates are ready.
- [ ] Weekly review cadence: a regular review time is set.
- [ ] Stop conditions: diagnosis, selection, performance, ranking, mandatory
  employer use, unsafe conflict, health/legal/occupational-health issues, and
  accidental sensitive disclosure responses are defined.
- [ ] No overbuilding: no auth, accounts, payments, backend APIs, databases,
  external analytics, cookies, tracking scripts, third-party services, or live
  form submission have been added.

If any item creates a privacy, safety, or misuse concern, pause beta invitation
until the concern is handled.
