# Onboarding

This guide helps a new user or beta participant start with the **High-Complexity
Contribution Tool** / **Instrument Complexiteit naar Bijdrage**.

The current package is a Markdown-first workbook and static-site prototype. It
does not include accounts, payments, backend storage, live beta submission,
external analytics providers, real form submission, or automated
interpretation.

## 1. Purpose

Use this guide to choose a safe starting path, understand the minimum reading,
and decide whether to use the workbook privately, with a coach, with a manager,
or only as a product review.

Onboarding should help users:

- Understand that the tool is non-diagnostic and non-scoring.
- Choose the right entry path.
- Avoid oversharing private workbook content.
- Focus on work examples, patterns, hypotheses, and role-design experiments.
- Know when to pause, involve a facilitator, or use another professional route.

The tool is for structured role-design conversation support. It is not for
diagnosis, IQ or giftedness proof, hiring, promotion, performance review,
employee ranking, support entitlement, legal advice, clinical advice,
occupational-health determinations, or formal HR decisions.

## 2. Choose Your Path

| Path | Start with | Main output | Use when | Do not use for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Individual user | `docs/WORKBOOK_v1.md` | Private pattern notes and a possible role-design request | You want clarity before deciding whether to involve anyone else. | Proving giftedness, diagnosing yourself, or preparing a demand. |
| Adult preparing for manager conversation | `docs/WORKBOOK_v1.md` and `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md` | Manager-safe summary and one practical ask | You have enough trust to discuss a bounded work experiment. | Sharing the full private workbook or sensitive personal details. |
| Coach or facilitator | `docs/USER_GUIDE.md`, `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`, and `docs/DEMO_FLOW.md` | Session plan, boundaries, and a safe route to an experiment or pause | You are supporting reflection or a role-design conversation. | Assessment, certification, diagnosis, scoring, or client-detail collection. |
| Manager or HR participant | `docs/MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md` and `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md` | Role/context notes and a possible manager action | You are examining role conditions with voluntary adult involvement. | Performance rating, selection, ranking, discipline, or support entitlement. |
| Employer pilot sponsor | `docs/BETA_PLAN.md`, `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md`, and `docs/TRUST_AND_LAUNCH_RISK_REVIEW.md` | Go/no-go view of pilot scope and safeguards | You are considering a very bounded voluntary pilot later. | Broad rollout, mandatory program, employee comparison, or HR decision workflow. |
| Product or beta reviewer | `docs/DEMO_FLOW.md`, `docs/ONBOARDING.md`, and `docs/MARKETING.md` | Product feedback on clarity, usefulness, safety, and missing artifacts | You are reviewing the package before real use. | Treating beta feedback as validation or collecting personal case evidence. |

If you are not sure which path fits, start with the demo flow and responsible
use guide before touching the workbook.

## 3. First 15 Minutes

Use the first 15 minutes to orient, not to complete the tool.

Read first:

1. The opening sections of [`README.md`](../README.md).
2. The boundaries in [`RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`](RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md).
3. The quick path in [`DEMO_FLOW.md`](DEMO_FLOW.md).
4. The "Purpose and Boundaries" section of [`WORKBOOK_v1.md`](WORKBOOK_v1.md)
   if you are an individual user.
5. The opening sections of
   [`MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md`](MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md) if
   you are a manager or HR participant.

Avoid in the first 15 minutes:

- Completing every workbook field.
- Sharing private material with a manager.
- Entering personal information into the static site form.
- Treating the example as a proven method or validated case.
- Deciding that the tool is the right route for a legal, clinical,
  occupational-health, disciplinary, or formal HR situation.

Decision to make next:

- Use privately.
- Use with a coach or facilitator.
- Prepare a manager-safe summary.
- Review as a product/beta reviewer only.
- Pause because the situation is out of scope or not safe enough.

## 4. First Full Use Cycle

A full cycle moves from preparation to review. It can be completed alone only
for private reflection, but manager-supported use needs voluntary participation
and clear boundaries.

| Phase | User action | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Preparation | Confirm the use is in scope, voluntary, and not a diagnosis or employment decision. | Go, narrow, facilitate, or pause. |
| Workbook | Complete selected sections of `docs/WORKBOOK_v1.md`, focusing on concrete examples. | Private notes, strongest signal, largest fit gap. |
| Interpretation | Use `docs/INTERPRETATION_GUIDE_v1.md` to identify patterns and alternative explanations. | One role-design hypothesis, not a conclusion. |
| Conversation | Use `docs/MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md` if a manager or facilitator is involved. | Manager-safe discussion and agreed scope. |
| 30-day experiment | Define one bounded work change with adult action, manager action, workload protection, evidence, stop conditions, and review date. | Experiment plan. |
| Review | Review contribution, clarity, energy, collaboration, hidden workload, and learning after 30 days. | Continue, adjust, try a different experiment, escalate, or pause. |

Partial completion is acceptable. A useful output can be one concrete example,
one safe request, and one experiment hypothesis.

## 5. Individual Onboarding

Use the workbook privately first.

Recommended path:

1. Read the purpose and boundaries in `docs/WORKBOOK_v1.md`.
2. Complete only the current work situation and the two or three dimensions
   that feel most relevant.
3. Write concrete work examples rather than identity statements.
4. Mark what stays private before drafting any manager-facing text.
5. Use the manager-safe summary structure from
   `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md`.
6. Decide whether the next step is private reflection, coaching, a manager
   conversation, or pause.

How to avoid over-sharing:

- Do not share the full private workbook by default.
- Remove labels, health details, diagnosis information, IQ or test information,
  full conflict narratives, and third-party personal details.
- Share only the work example, role condition, practical request, review
  evidence, and stop condition needed for the conversation.
- Ask for facilitation if trust, power dynamics, or conflict level make direct
  sharing risky.

How to decide whether to involve a manager:

- Involve a manager only when the conversation can stay voluntary, practical,
  and role/context oriented.
- Use a coach or facilitator first if you need help narrowing the request.
- Do not involve a manager through this tool if the situation is adversarial,
  disciplinary, legally sensitive, health-dominated, or unsafe.

## 6. Manager-Supported Onboarding

Manager-supported use should begin with a clear frame.

Suggested framing:

> This is a role-design conversation. The goal is to inspect work conditions
> and test one bounded experiment, not to diagnose, rate, rank, or decide
> anything about the person.

How to keep the adult in control:

- The adult decides what private material is shared.
- The manager does not request the private workbook.
- The conversation uses work examples and role/context conditions.
- Documentation is limited to agreed actions, review evidence, stop
  conditions, and operational commitments.
- The adult can pause or narrow the conversation.

How to use the manager guide:

- Read "How to Enter the Conversation" before the meeting.
- Use the 30-minute version only if preparation and trust are already present.
- Use the 60-minute version when the pattern needs more careful exploration.
- Ask manager-safe questions from the question bank.
- Define one experiment rather than redesigning the entire role.

What not to document:

- Completed private self-scan responses.
- Diagnostic labels, IQ, giftedness proof, health details, disability details,
  burnout details, treatment history, or occupational-health material.
- Full conflict narratives.
- Personal interpretations about character, capability, motivation, or mental
  health.
- Third-party information about colleagues, clients, or managers.
- Any conclusion about hiring, promotion, performance, ranking, support
  entitlement, discipline, or formal accommodation.

## 7. Coach/Facilitator Onboarding

Coaches, advisors, HR partners, and facilitators should prepare the process
before reviewing personal material.

How to prepare:

- Read `docs/USER_GUIDE.md`, `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`,
  `docs/INTERPRETATION_GUIDE_v1.md`, and
  `docs/MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md`.
- Run the quick demo in `docs/DEMO_FLOW.md`.
- Decide whether the situation is safe enough for ordinary workbook use.
- Clarify note-taking, storage, deletion, and sharing boundaries.
- Ask the adult what should stay private before any joint session.

How to set boundaries:

- Present the tool as structured reflection and role-design support.
- Say explicitly that it is not a validated assessment, diagnosis, score,
  certification route, or employment decision tool.
- Keep the output as patterns, hypotheses, role levers, and experiments.
- Pause if the conversation moves into health, legal, disciplinary,
  occupational-health, disability, accommodation, or crisis territory.

How to handle strong reactions:

- Slow down and narrow the topic.
- Move from labels to concrete work examples.
- Ask what can safely be shared and what should remain private.
- Separate role-design questions from formal support routes.
- Do not force completion of a worksheet.

How to collect feedback:

- Ask about clarity, usefulness, safety, missing artifacts, and wording.
- Do not collect completed scans, client notes, sensitive stories, or
  identifiable workplace details.
- Summarize feedback as product-learning themes.
- Ask separate permission before using any quote.

## 8. Employer Pilot Onboarding

Employer pilots are not ready by default. They require stronger safeguards than
individual or coaching use.

Entry conditions:

- Participation is voluntary.
- Non-participation has no adverse consequence.
- The adult controls what private material is shared.
- The pilot purpose, participant count, scope, timing, outputs, data handling,
  stop conditions, and owner are written down.
- The tool is not used for hiring, promotion, selection, ranking, performance,
  discipline, support entitlement, surveillance, or employee comparison.
- A facilitator or neutral process owner is used when power dynamics or
  conflict risk matter.

Documents to review:

- `docs/BETA_PLAN.md`
- `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md`
- `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`
- `docs/MANAGER_CONVERSATION_GUIDE_v1.md`
- `docs/TRUST_AND_LAUNCH_RISK_REVIEW.md`
- `docs/TERMS_AND_USE_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/PRIVACY_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/SECURITY.md`

Scope boundaries:

- Use one small group or one bounded conversation, not a rollout.
- Use blank materials only.
- Do not collect completed private workbooks centrally.
- Keep employer records limited to agreed role-design actions and review
  evidence.
- Do not use outputs in employment decisions.

Consent and disclosure expectations:

- Explain the beta status before use.
- Confirm no diagnosis, assessment, ranking, or performance use.
- Confirm what is documented, where it is kept, who sees it, and when it is
  deleted or reviewed.
- Provide a pause or exit option.

Pilot checklist:

- [ ] Written purpose.
- [ ] Voluntary participation statement.
- [ ] No-adverse-consequence statement.
- [ ] Adult disclosure control.
- [ ] Documentation boundary.
- [ ] Data handling boundary.
- [ ] Facilitator or process owner.
- [ ] Stop conditions.
- [ ] 30-day review plan.
- [ ] No HR decision use.

## 9. Safety and Responsible-Use Reminders

Keep these reminders visible:

- Use signals, patterns, hypotheses, and role-design experiments.
- Do not use scores, profiles, labels, diagnosis, rankings, or assessment
  results.
- The adult controls disclosure.
- Completed scans may contain sensitive information.
- Manager notes should stay operational.
- Workload protection and stop conditions belong in every experiment.
- Pause when the issue is legal, clinical, disciplinary, occupational-health,
  formal HR, disability, accommodation, or unsafe conflict.
