# Roadmap

## 1. Roadmap Purpose

This roadmap stages development of the **High-Complexity Contribution Tool** /
**Instrument Complexiteit naar Bijdrage** through evidence-gated steps.

Movement between stages should depend on observed clarity, responsible use,
privacy control, product usefulness, and commercial learning. Interest alone is
not enough to advance.

The roadmap does not authorize public launch, payments, accounts, backend
services, data collection, employer rollout, or stronger claims.

## 2. Current Status

Current status:

- Private-beta prototype.
- Markdown/static-site package.
- Manual beta operation.
- Static site with no input fields, no backend, no storage, no mailing-list
  integration, no external analytics, no checkout, and no real submission.
- Static deployment planning docs exist, but no external deployment has been
  performed.
- Evidence-informed but not validated as a commercial, diagnostic,
  psychometric, clinical, HR, legal, or occupational-health instrument.

Current strongest use:

- Internal review.
- Trusted private-beta review.
- Small invite-only individual beta when the SOP is followed.
- Product/commercial review without live payments or public claims.

Current gated uses:

- Facilitated beta.
- Employer pilot.
- Paid pilot.
- Public launch.
- SaaS or account-based version.

## 3. Stage 0: Internal Review

Goals:

- Confirm that the package is coherent enough for trusted review.
- Find confusing navigation, stale wording, overclaiming, broken local links,
  and responsible-use gaps.
- Verify that no live data collection, payment, account, backend, or external
  analytics behavior exists.

Required artifacts:

- README.
- Core workbook, interpretation guide, manager guide, and user guide.
- Package index and documentation index.
- Responsible-use guide.
- Beta disclosure.
- Privacy, security, and terms placeholders.
- Trust/risk and launch readiness reviews.
- Private-beta release notes.
- Launch checklist.

Exit criteria:

- Current v1 docs are the clear entry path.
- Legacy v0.1 docs are labeled as reference only.
- Private-beta blocker fixes are documented.
- Static site wording does not imply live intake or public launch.
- Open items are captured rather than hidden.

## 4. Stage 1: Private Individual Beta

Goals:

- Test whether individual adults and trusted reviewers recognize the problem.
- Test whether the workbook helps users choose concrete examples and sharing
  boundaries.
- Test whether the non-diagnostic, non-selection, and no-sensitive-feedback
  boundaries are understood.

Required artifacts:

- `docs/BETA_DISCLOSURE_AND_CONSENT.md`
- `docs/PRIVATE_BETA_RECORDS_SOP.md`
- `docs/BETA_PLAN.md`
- `docs/RESPONSIBLE_USE_GUIDE.md`
- `docs/PRIVACY_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/SECURITY.md`
- `docs/TERMS_AND_USE_PLACEHOLDER.md`
- `docs/WORKBOOK_v1.md`
- `docs/INTERPRETATION_GUIDE_v1.md`
- `docs/USER_GUIDE.md`

What to test:

- Clarity of the problem framing.
- Usability of the workbook.
- Whether users can form a bounded 30-day experiment or responsible pause.
- Whether adults feel in control of what stays private.
- Whether feedback can remain product-focused.
- Whether first-use navigation is too heavy.

Exit criteria:

- At least several trusted users give structured feedback without unmanaged
  sensitive disclosure.
- Users can explain what the tool is not for.
- Misuse or over-disclosure risks are logged and addressed.
- The maintainer can operate contact, consent, feedback, deletion, and weekly
  learning records manually.

## 5. Stage 2: Facilitated Beta

Goals:

- Test whether coaches, advisors, HR partners, or facilitators can use the
  package responsibly.
- Learn what scripts, handouts, examples, and session boundaries are missing.
- Assess whether a professional/facilitator pack is a credible early paid
  direction.

What to test:

- Session flow.
- Missing facilitator scripts.
- Pause, decline, referral, and accidental-disclosure handling.
- Whether manager-safe summaries work.
- Whether interpretation stays qualitative and role/context oriented.
- Whether professionals overclaim, request certification, or need stronger
  license boundaries.

Additional materials needed:

- Facilitator guide or session script.
- Separate 30-day tracker and review handout.
- Filled interpretation example.
- Boundary and referral scripts.
- Lightweight claims register before paid/public copy.

Exit criteria:

- Facilitators can use the materials without author explanation.
- Several uses produce a concrete experiment or responsible pause.
- Product feedback identifies repeatable package improvements.
- No persistent drift into diagnosis, scoring, performance, selection, or
  certification claims occurs.

## 6. Stage 3: Bounded Employer Pilot

Preconditions:

- Earlier private/facilitated beta shows manageable privacy and misuse risk.
- Written employer pilot scope exists.
- Voluntary participation and no-adverse-consequence language is explicit.
- Adult control over sharing is accepted.
- No completed workbooks are stored in HR files.
- A facilitator or neutral process owner is available where power dynamics are
  material.

Scope:

- One to two tightly bounded employer or manager conversations.
- Role/context conditions only.
- One 30-day experiment or responsible pause.
- No rollout, dashboard, employee comparison, scoring, or HR decision support.

Required agreement/scope doc:

- Purpose.
- Participant count.
- Time box.
- Outputs.
- Data handling.
- Documentation boundaries.
- Owner.
- Stop conditions.
- Review date.
- No hiring, promotion, performance, ranking, support-entitlement, diagnosis,
  or employee-comparison use.

Responsible-use controls:

- Participation is voluntary.
- Declining or pausing has no adverse consequence.
- Adult private workbook content stays private unless the adult chooses to
  share a limited summary.
- Employer notes stay role/context-only and minimum necessary.
- Pilot feedback excludes personal case detail.

Exit criteria:

- Employer accepts the boundaries in writing.
- The conversation remains role/context oriented.
- Any output is an experiment or responsible pause, not a person judgment.
- Data boundaries hold.
- Repeat use would not require weaker safeguards.

## 7. Stage 4: First Paid Pilot

Preconditions:

- Specific paid offer is defined.
- The package contents are explicit.
- Delivery, support, terms, privacy, refund/cancellation, license scope, and
  payment/invoice handling can be handled manually.
- Buyers accept responsible-use boundaries.
- Pricing interest is specific and credible enough to test.

Packaging choices:

- Individual workbook.
- Professional/facilitator pack.
- Founder-led facilitated session.
- Small workshop.
- Bounded employer pilot.

Payment approach candidates:

- Manual invoice or bank transfer for early professional packs, workshops, and
  pilots.
- Hosted checkout only after terms, privacy, support, refund, license, tax/VAT,
  and delivery flows are ready.
- No account portal unless update access or support volume justifies it.

Trust/legal/privacy prerequisites:

- Reviewed terms and privacy materials for the paid data flow.
- Refund and cancellation notes.
- Support process.
- License scope.
- Payment/invoice/tax handling.
- No collection of completed workbook responses through payment, support, or
  feedback routes.

Exit criteria:

- A buyer pays or commits through a responsible manual route.
- The offer can be delivered without overclaiming or privacy creep.
- Support burden is understood.
- Paid feedback informs the package rather than pushing it toward assessment,
  ranking, or automation.

## 8. Stage 5: Public Launch Candidate

Preconditions:

- Private, facilitated, and any paid-pilot learning support a public package.
- Public package contents are frozen.
- Final privacy, terms, security/contact, support, refund, license, and claims
  materials are reviewed.
- Static deployment target is selected and tested.
- Deployment plan and deployment QA checklist are reviewed.
- Public vs private-link hosting, docs exposure, and indexing decisions are
  made.
- Any live form, payment, analytics, download, or support service has a
  documented data-flow and retention review.

Required production work:

- Public deployment.
- Domain.
- Deployment QA.
- Rollback plan.
- Link QA.
- Accessibility QA.
- Mobile spot-checks.
- Hosting/security-header review.
- Contact/support route.
- Final privacy policy.
- Final terms.
- Claims review.
- Payment decision if selling.
- Analytics decision if measuring.
- Download/delivery process if distributing files.

Validation and claims discipline:

- Keep evidence wording modest unless stronger evidence has been reviewed.
- Do not imply diagnostic, predictive, normed, clinical, legal, HR, or outcome
  validation.
- Use beta evidence only for usability, clarity, responsible-use learning, and
  commercial interest unless a reviewed validation study exists.

Website/deployment/payment/privacy requirements:

- Public pages must not imply invite-only beta is public self-serve access.
- Payment claims must match a real payment process.
- Any live form must have privacy notice, consent, retention, deletion, and
  support handling.
- Any analytics must avoid completed workbook content and sensitive event
  properties.

## 9. Later Possibilities

Later possibilities, only if justified by evidence and responsible-use needs:

- Download delivery.
- Payment flow.
- Account portal.
- Facilitator pack.
- Employer workshop.
- Bilingual NL/EN version.
- Research validation.
- SaaS version only if justified.
- Export-ready PDF/DOCX packages.
- Public sample pages or free starter artifact.
- Professional update access.

These should not be built merely to make the package look more like software.

## 10. Explicit Non-Goals for Now

The product is not being built as:

- Diagnosis.
- IQ test.
- Giftedness proof.
- Psychometric assessment.
- Hiring tool.
- Promotion tool.
- Performance-rating tool.
- Employee-ranking tool.
- High-potential ranking tool.
- Support-entitlement tool.
- Clinical, medical, legal, occupational-health, disability, or formal
  accommodation instrument.
- Automated interpretation or recommendation engine.
- Employee analytics dashboard.

## 11. Decision Gates

Evidence needed to move between stages:

- Users understand the non-diagnostic and non-selection boundaries.
- Adults can keep private information private.
- Feedback remains product-focused.
- Role-design experiments are concrete and bounded.
- Misuse attempts are rare, clear, and manageable.
- The maintainer can operate the records process without collecting sensitive
  case material.
- Commercial interest is specific enough to test responsibly.

What would trigger a pivot:

- Most interest seeks diagnosis, proof, validation, certification, ranking,
  selection, or HR decision support.
- Adults feel pressured to disclose or share completed workbooks.
- Employers resist voluntary participation or documentation boundaries.
- Professionals overclaim the method as an assessment.
- The workbook cannot produce concrete experiments without heavy custom
  facilitation.
- Privacy or support burden is too high for the current package.

What would pause employer use:

- Any request for hiring, promotion, performance, ranking, support-entitlement,
  discipline, surveillance, or employee comparison.
- Any pressure on adults to participate or disclose.
- Any attempt to store completed private workbooks in HR files.
- Any unclear data handling, retention, or access route.
- Any unsafe conflict, legal, occupational-health, clinical, disability, or
  formal accommodation context requiring specialist support.
