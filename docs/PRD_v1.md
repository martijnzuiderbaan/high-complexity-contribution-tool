# PRD v1

Prepared for the v1 launch of the **High-Complexity Contribution Tool** / **Instrument Complexiteit naar Bijdrage**.

This PRD assumes the current product is a guided workbook and facilitation package built from the existing Markdown artifacts:

- `Adult Self-Scan v0.1.md`
- `Employer Role Scan v0.1.md`
- `Joint Conversation Canvas v0.1.md`

The product must remain non-diagnostic, non-selective, and role-design oriented. It must not become an IQ test, giftedness diagnosis, psychometric assessment, hiring tool, promotion tool, performance-review tool, burnout diagnosis, occupational-health determination, disability determination, or automated employee-ranking system.

## 1. Product one-liner

The High-Complexity Contribution Tool helps high-complexity adults, managers, and facilitators turn vague role-fit tension into a safe, concrete 30-day work experiment that can unlock sustainable contribution without diagnosis, scoring, or HR ranking.

## 2. Target users

### Primary user

Coaches, career counselors, HR partners, independent facilitators, and people-development professionals who already work with gifted, hoogbegaafde, neurodivergent, fast-learning, or high-complexity adults and need a structured, careful way to guide role-fit conversations.

Why this is the primary launch user:

- They are the most likely early adopters.
- They can interpret sensitive material responsibly.
- They can pay for a professional workbook/license.
- They can help prevent misuse by employers or over-disclosure by adults.
- They can produce feedback from real facilitated use.

### Secondary users

- Gifted, hoogbegaafde, or high-complexity adults who feel underused, overloaded, misunderstood, constrained, or near conflict/exit in work.
- Managers or employers who want to improve role conditions without turning the situation into diagnosis, special pleading, performance judgment, or talent ranking.
- HR teams that need a responsible-use framework for role design, contribution, learning, autonomy, quality dialogue, sparring, translation, energy, and boundaries.

### Excluded users

The v1 product is not for:

- Anyone seeking proof of giftedness, IQ measurement, diagnosis, or psychometric scoring.
- Employers seeking hiring, selection, promotion, performance, high-potential, or ranking instruments.
- Managers looking for a shortcut to decide whether someone deserves support.
- Clinical, legal, occupational-health, disability, or formal accommodation decisions.
- Unsafe, adversarial, disciplinary, or legally sensitive situations without appropriate specialist support.
- Automated analytics vendors or HR dashboards that want to process completed personal responses.

## 3. Core problem

### Painful workflow this replaces

The current alternative is usually an improvised and risky conversation:

- The adult tries to explain underchallenge, overdrive, values friction, or misfit without sounding entitled, difficult, fragile, or abstract.
- The manager sees behavior, friction, boredom, speed, quality sensitivity, or conflict risk but lacks a safe role-design language.
- HR or coaches rely on generic engagement tools, personality labels, performance processes, or informal advice that does not fit high-complexity contribution.
- The conversation drifts toward proving giftedness, diagnosing the person, debating attitude, or escalating conflict.
- The outcome is often vague: "communicate better", "be more flexible", "give more challenge", or "set boundaries", without a testable role experiment.

The tool replaces this with a three-part workflow:

1. Private adult self-reflection.
2. Employer-side role/context scan.
3. Joint conversation canvas that ends in one bounded 30-day experiment.

### Why users would care enough to try it

Users care because the cost of mishandling this situation is high:

- Adults may experience bore-out, hidden potential, overdrive, conflict, or exit risk.
- Managers may lose scarce cognitive capacity or misread useful complexity as resistance.
- Coaches and HR partners may spend too much time translating between personal experience and role design.
- Organizations may waste talent by offering too little complexity, too little autonomy, poor sparring, or unsustainable load.

The product gives users a way to move from blame and labels to concrete role levers: complexity lanes, decision zones, learning sprints, quality-dialogue formats, sparring routes, translation formats, meeting/focus-time changes, and explicit stop conditions.

### Why users might pay

Users may pay because the paid version saves preparation time, reduces facilitation risk, and produces a professional artifact they can use in real work:

- Coaches/facilitators get a ready-to-run session structure, handouts, examples, and responsible-use guidance.
- HR partners get language that is safer than informal giftedness debates or generic performance processes.
- Adults get a private preparation tool that helps them choose what to share and what to keep private.
- Managers get practical role levers rather than abstract personality interpretation.
- Organizations can reduce avoidable churn, conflict, underuse, and overdrive by testing bounded role changes before situations escalate.

## 4. Main use cases

### Use case 1: Adult prepares a safer work conversation

User story:

> As a high-complexity adult, I want to prepare my observations and disclosure boundaries before speaking with my manager, so that I can ask for a practical role experiment without oversharing or turning the conversation into a giftedness debate.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | The adult feels underused, overloaded, misunderstood, constrained, or near conflict/exit. |
| Action | Complete the Adult Self-Scan privately, choose concrete examples, mark what to keep private, and draft a shareable conversation summary. |
| Output | A concise summary of strongest contribution, largest fit gap, safe request, 30-day experiment candidate, and evidence of success/failure. |
| Success criteria | The adult can name one fit dimension, one concrete example, one safe request, and one 30-day experiment without needing to disclose private labels or health-adjacent details. |

### Use case 2: Manager examines role/context before reacting to the person

User story:

> As a manager, I want to inspect the role and context conditions around a high-complexity employee, so that I can adjust contribution conditions rather than judging the person too quickly.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | A manager sees boredom, overdrive, friction, fast mastery, quality concerns, or untapped contribution. |
| Action | Complete the Employer Role Scan, assess the six fit dimensions and leadership/context conditions, and identify adjustable role levers. |
| Output | A role-design output with current contribution, blocked/overused/hidden capacity, possible adjustments, legitimate constraints, avoidable process noise, and improvement evidence. |
| Success criteria | The manager can propose one realistic role/context adjustment and distinguish legitimate constraints from avoidable friction. |

### Use case 3: Facilitator runs a structured joint session

User story:

> As a facilitator, I want a session flow that protects agency and keeps both sides grounded in evidence, so that the conversation ends in one bounded experiment rather than diagnosis, blame, or broad redesign.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | Adult and manager agree to discuss role fit with a coach, HR partner, or facilitator. |
| Action | Use the Joint Conversation Canvas to compare adult and employer signals, select one pathway hypothesis, and define a 30-day work experiment. |
| Output | A completed joint canvas with shared evidence, selected fit dimension, pathway hypothesis, adult actions, manager actions, protected workload, stop conditions, and review date. |
| Success criteria | The session ends with one experiment that is small, observable, time-bound, safe enough to run, and explicitly not a performance or giftedness assessment. |

### Use case 4: HR or coach evaluates whether this is the right tool

User story:

> As an HR partner or coach, I want a clear intake and responsible-use screen, so that I can decide whether to use this tool, refer elsewhere, or pause because the situation is unsafe or out of scope.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | A case includes high complexity, underuse, conflict, burnout risk, role mismatch, or manager-employee tension. |
| Action | Review the product boundaries, privacy/disclosure guide, and "when not to use" guidance before starting. |
| Output | A go/no-go decision: use the tool, use it only with facilitation, refer to HR/legal/occupational health/clinical support, or pause. |
| Success criteria | The tool is not used when diagnosis, legal action, formal accommodation, disciplinary action, or occupational-health determination is the actual need. |

### Use case 5: Team reviews a 30-day experiment

User story:

> As an adult-manager pair, I want to review a role experiment after 30 days, so that we can continue, adjust, formalize, escalate, or stop based on evidence.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | A 30-day experiment reaches its review date. |
| Action | Complete the review section of the Joint Conversation Canvas and compare expected evidence with observed contribution, clarity, energy, collaboration, and hidden workload. |
| Output | A next decision: continue, adjust, convert into role agreement, escalate to a specialist route, or conclude that the role cannot be redesigned enough. |
| Success criteria | The decision is based on concrete evidence, includes both adult and manager learning, and does not become a retroactive performance judgment. |

## 5. v1 feature scope

### Free/demo features

Free materials should create trust, explain the model, and help users decide whether the paid workbook is relevant.

- Landing page with product one-liner, audience paths, workflow overview, and boundaries.
- Free mini-canvas or conversation checklist.
- Sample pages from the Adult Self-Scan, Employer Role Scan, and Joint Conversation Canvas.
- Plain-language responsible-use note.
- Short "when not to use this tool" guide.
- FAQ covering diagnosis, IQ, giftedness labels, privacy, HR misuse, burnout, accommodations, and facilitation.
- Email capture for updates, beta invitations, and professional pack interest.

### Paid features

Paid materials should be practical, complete, and usable in real sessions.

- v1 Adult Self-Scan workbook.
- v1 Employer Role Scan workbook.
- v1 Joint Conversation Canvas.
- Adult privacy and disclosure guide.
- Employer responsible-use guide.
- Facilitator guide with session flow, scripts, timing, and intervention points.
- One or two synthetic worked examples.
- 30-day experiment tracker and review template.
- Professional handout pack for coaches, HR, and facilitators.
- Printable PDF and editable Markdown/DOCX versions if export quality is ready.
- Version updates for paying professional users during the v1 cycle.

### Admin/internal features

These are internal product operations, not end-user assessment features.

- Versioned source files and release notes.
- Content QA checklist for non-diagnostic boundaries, privacy language, terminology consistency, and frontmatter shape.
- Basic Markdown/link/heading checks if automation is added later.
- Feedback intake form for beta/professional users.
- Support inbox or contact route.
- Privacy-minimal sales and download tracking.
- Internal review log for changes to boundary language and responsible-use guidance.

### Explicitly out of scope

- IQ testing or giftedness diagnosis.
- Psychometric scoring or normed assessment claims.
- Automated interpretation of completed personal responses.
- Hiring, selection, promotion, performance, high-potential, or ranking decisions.
- Burnout diagnosis, clinical advice, occupational-health determinations, disability determinations, or legal advice.
- Employee ranking dashboards or manager decision dashboards.
- Analytics on completed personal responses.
- Uploading or storing completed scans in a central product system.
- Any recommendation engine that bypasses human judgment and context.
- A full web app for v1 unless the workbook/package has first been validated.

## 6. UX flow

The v1 UX is a workbook and landing-page flow, not an application flow.

### First-time user flow

1. User lands on the website or README.
2. User sees the promise: convert complex capacity into sustainable contribution through a structured role-design conversation.
3. User selects a path: adult, manager/employer, coach/facilitator, or HR.
4. User reads visible boundaries: not diagnostic, not scoring, not HR decision support, not medical/legal/occupational-health advice.
5. User previews sample pages or downloads the free checklist.
6. User either buys/downloads the workbook or requests a facilitated/professional option.
7. Adult completes the Adult Self-Scan privately and chooses what not to share.
8. Manager/employer completes the Employer Role Scan from the role/context side.
9. Both sides use the Joint Conversation Canvas, preferably with a facilitator when the situation is sensitive.
10. The conversation ends with one 30-day experiment, stop conditions, protected workload, and a review date.
11. After 30 days, users complete the review and choose continue, adjust, formalize, escalate, or stop.

### Returning user flow

1. User returns with a new role-fit question, a continuing experiment, or a new client/session.
2. User opens the relevant artifact: Adult Self-Scan, Employer Role Scan, Joint Canvas, or 30-day review.
3. User copies forward only what is still relevant and safe to use.
4. User updates evidence, role constraints, and experiment scope.
5. User runs a new or adjusted 30-day experiment.
6. Facilitators may reuse the professional pack with multiple clients under the license terms.

### Error states

Because v1 is a workbook, "error states" are workflow failure states that need explicit guidance.

| State | Product response |
|---|---|
| Adult does not feel safe sharing | Use the private self-scan only, share a minimal request, involve a facilitator, or pause. |
| Employer wants to use the tool for performance or selection | Stop use and redirect to responsible-use boundaries; this is out of scope. |
| Health, burnout, disability, legal, or occupational-health issue dominates | Refer to the appropriate specialist route before using or continuing the tool. |
| Situation is adversarial, disciplinary, or legally sensitive | Do not run an ordinary joint canvas; require specialist support or pause. |
| No concrete examples are available | Use the tool as reflection only; collect work examples before a joint session. |
| Role cannot realistically be changed | Use the review decision path to stop, escalate, or explore fit outside the current role. |
| Experiment would add hidden workload | Redesign with explicit pause/reduce/protect elements, or do not run it. |
| Manager and adult disagree sharply about evidence | Narrow the experiment, use observable evidence, and consider facilitation. |
| User wants a score or label | Refuse that framing and return to person-role-context fit and concrete work evidence. |

### Empty states

| State | Product response |
|---|---|
| Blank Adult Self-Scan | Offer example prompts and remind the adult that partial completion is acceptable. |
| Blank Employer Role Scan | Start with one role lever: complexity lane, decision zones, learning sprint, quality dialogue, sparring route, or focus-time review. |
| No safe topic to share | Use the disclosure planner and prepare a minimal request without personal labels. |
| No employer participant | Use the adult workbook for self-clarity, coach preparation, or career/role decision support only. |
| No clear experiment | Select one fit dimension and one small change; avoid full role redesign. |
| No improvement after 30 days | Review evidence, check hidden workload, adjust or stop rather than blaming the person. |

## 7. Business model

### Recommended initial monetization model

Use a workbook-first model with a professional license and optional services layer.

Recommended structure:

1. Free lead magnet: mini-canvas, checklist, sample pages, and responsible-use note.
2. Paid individual workbook: adult scan, disclosure guide, joint canvas, and 30-day tracker.
3. Professional pack/license: full facilitator-ready toolkit for coaches, HR partners, and people-development professionals.
4. Organization pilot: responsible-use package plus facilitated workshop or implementation support.
5. Services layer: facilitated sessions, manager training, coach enablement, and custom implementation.

Avoid per-employee assessment pricing. The product should be sold as a role-design and facilitation tool, not as an assessment instrument.

### Suggested pricing tiers

Initial pricing should be validated during private beta, but a practical starting structure is:

| Tier | Price suggestion | Buyer | Includes |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Free starter | EUR 0 | Adults, managers, coaches | Overview, mini-canvas, sample pages, responsible-use note, newsletter/beta signup. |
| Individual workbook | EUR 39-79 one-time | Adult or manager | Adult Self-Scan, Joint Canvas, disclosure guide, 30-day experiment tracker, review template. |
| Professional pack | EUR 249-499 per year or EUR 299-699 one-time for early v1 | Coaches, HR partners, facilitators | Full artifact set, employer scan, facilitator guide, synthetic examples, handouts, session scripts, updates. |
| Organization pilot | EUR 1,500-5,000 per pilot | Small team, HR, leadership group | Professional pack, responsible-use onboarding, workshop, pilot feedback session, limited internal use rights. |
| Facilitation/service | Custom | Individuals or organizations | Guided session, manager training, role-fit workshop, implementation support. |

### What should be free

- Product overview and clear one-liner.
- Boundary and responsible-use guidance.
- "When not to use this tool" guidance.
- A free checklist or mini-canvas.
- Sample pages that show the format and non-diagnostic stance.
- FAQ and basic privacy/disclosure guidance.

### What should require payment

- Complete workbook bundle.
- Facilitator guide and professional session scripts.
- Employer responsible-use guide in full.
- Synthetic worked examples.
- Editable templates.
- 30-day tracker and review templates.
- Professional license and update access.
- Organization onboarding and facilitated use.

### Why this model fits the current product

The current repository is a content product, not a software platform. A workbook-first business model fits because:

- The existing value is in framing, structure, questions, safeguards, and workflow.
- The audit shows the fastest useful v1 is a polished guided workbook.
- A professional license matches the likely early adopter: coaches, HR partners, and facilitators.
- It preserves human judgment and avoids automated assessment risk.
- It can launch before building software, while still creating revenue and feedback.

## 9. Metrics

### Activation metric

Primary activation:

- A user reaches a concrete 30-day experiment draft with a selected fit dimension, work hypothesis, adult action, manager action, protected workload element, evidence of success/failure, stop conditions, and review date.

Early proxy activation:

- Free checklist downloaded and audience path selected.
- Workbook purchased and first artifact opened/downloaded.
- Professional user completes a session plan with the facilitator guide.

### Retention metric

Primary retention:

- A user or facilitator runs a review after 30 days and either adjusts, continues, formalizes, or starts a second experiment.

Professional retention:

- Coach/HR/facilitator uses the tool with a second client/team or renews/update-downloads the professional pack.

### Revenue metric

Primary revenue metric:

- Monthly paid workbook/professional-pack revenue.

Supporting revenue metrics:

- Free-to-paid conversion rate.
- Professional-pack conversion rate from professional interest leads.
- Organization pilot bookings.
- Average revenue per professional buyer.
- Services revenue attached to product-led inquiries.

### Quality metric

Primary quality metric:

- Percentage of completed sessions that produce a bounded, non-diagnostic 30-day experiment and are rated by users as clear, safe, and useful.

Safeguard metrics:

- Number of reported misuse attempts, such as scoring, diagnosis, performance use, or ranking.
- Percentage of users who understand what the tool is not for.
- Percentage of adults who report they could choose what to keep private.
- Percentage of experiments that include workload protection and stop conditions.
- Qualitative feedback on confusing wording, over-interpretation, or privacy concern.

Do not collect completed personal scan content for analytics unless a future product has explicit consent, data minimization, retention, and privacy design.
