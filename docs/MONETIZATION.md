# Monetization and Pricing Plan

This plan covers commercial packaging for the **High-Complexity Contribution
Tool** / **Instrument Complexiteit naar Bijdrage**.

It is a plan only. Checkout, payment, account access, license delivery,
analytics, beta capture, privacy pages, and terms are not live.

The product must remain a non-diagnostic role-design and conversation aid. It
must not be sold as an IQ test, giftedness diagnosis, psychometric assessment,
hiring tool, selection tool, promotion tool, performance-review tool, burnout
diagnosis, occupational-health determination, disability determination,
support-entitlement tool, automated ranking system, or employee decision system.

## 1. Commercial thesis

### What people are actually paying for

People are not paying for proof of giftedness or a score. They are paying for:

- A safer structure for a difficult work conversation.
- Language that turns complexity, learning speed, autonomy, quality concerns,
  sparring needs, translation work, intensity, and energy boundaries into work
  examples.
- A practical route from vague role-fit tension to one bounded 30-day
  experiment.
- Reduced preparation time for coaches, HR partners, facilitators, managers,
  and adults.
- Responsible-use safeguards that reduce over-disclosure, employer misuse,
  diagnosis drift, and performance framing.
- Professional materials that can be used in sessions, workshops, or pilots
  without the author explaining every step personally.

The paid value is workflow quality, trust, facilitation readiness, and risk
reduction.

### Why this tool is commercially plausible

The commercial opening is plausible because the underlying work problem is
expensive when handled poorly:

- Adults may be underused, overloaded, misunderstood, or near conflict or exit.
- Managers may see useful complexity as resistance, attitude, fragility,
  perfectionism, or inconsistency.
- Coaches and HR partners often need a concrete bridge between personal
  experience and role design.
- Employers may want retention and contribution without turning the topic into
  diagnosis, special pleading, or high-potential ranking.
- The current repository already contains a coherent workbook-first tool pack:
  a workbook, interpretation guide, manager conversation guide, user guide,
  package index, audit, PRD, and static landing page.

The strongest early buyer is likely not a cold self-serve employer. It is a
coach, HR partner, people-development professional, or founder-led early buyer
who already recognizes the pattern and needs a usable structure.

### Why website-first beta capture still makes sense

Website-first beta capture remains sensible because the product needs learning
before infrastructure:

- The static site can explain the job to be done and visible boundaries without
  collecting completed scans.
- Audience-path selection can show whether adults, coaches, HR, managers, or
  employers are most ready to engage.
- Beta requests can test language, urgency, willingness to pay, and misuse risk
  before payments are built.
- The website can route sensitive or employer-led interest into a manual review
  flow rather than immediate checkout.
- The current product is still a workbook and facilitation package, so a simple
  request-and-qualify path is safer than self-serve sales.

No beta capture should collect completed workbook responses, health details,
conflict details, HR case details, or identifiable employee information.

### What must be learned before scaling

Before scaling, learn:

- Users understand that the tool is non-diagnostic and not for HR decisions.
- Adults can use the workbook without oversharing private information.
- Coaches, HR partners, and facilitators can run the flow without author
  support.
- Managers can stay focused on role/context conditions rather than judging the
  person.
- A meaningful share of sessions end in one concrete, reviewable 30-day
  experiment.
- The language works for people who do not strongly identify with giftedness or
  hoogbegaafdheid.
- The product is concrete enough, not too abstract or sensitive for practical
  use.
- Buyers will pay for a workbook/professional pack, facilitation, or pilot
  before a full software product exists.
- Support burden and misuse risk remain manageable.
- Privacy, terms, refund, licensing, and EU/VAT handling can be made clear
  before live payments.

## 2. Recommended initial business model

Primary model: **facilitated professional pack, tested through founder-led
early use**.

This is a focused hybrid model. The paid center should be a professional pack
for coaches, HR partners, people-development professionals, and responsible
facilitators. Founder-led sessions and workshops should be used early to learn
what the package must contain. A paid individual workbook can exist as a
lower-priced secondary offer after the beta shows that adults can use it safely
without facilitation. Employer/team licenses should remain pilot-led until
responsible-use safeguards are stronger.

Why this model fits:

- Trust: the topic is sensitive. A professional or facilitated channel reduces
  over-disclosure and employer misuse.
- Buyer readiness: coaches, HR partners, and facilitators already pay for
  practical methods, templates, and session materials.
- Market education burden: professionals can explain the difference between
  role design, giftedness labels, health questions, and HR decisions.
- Evidence still needed: early facilitated use creates better feedback than
  anonymous downloads alone.
- Commercial realism: professional packs, workshops, and pilots can reach EUR
  1k to EUR 5k revenue faster than low-price individual downloads, without
  pretending beta learning is validation.

Why not make other models primary yet:

- Individual paid workbook: useful, but riskier as the first paid offer because
  some adults may need privacy guidance, facilitation, or escalation before
  sharing anything at work.
- Employer/team license: commercially attractive, but risky before
  responsible-use, terms, privacy, and facilitation guidance are mature.
- Workshop or advisory-led only: useful for validation, but it can become a
  consulting business unless the reusable professional pack is developed in
  parallel.
- Pure self-serve hybrid: too much misuse risk before buyer intent and safe use
  are better understood.

## 3. Buyer and user distinction

### Adult end user

The adult uses the tool for private reflection, disclosure planning,
conversation preparation, and a possible 30-day experiment. The adult may buy a
workbook, but should not be pushed into sharing completed scans with an employer
or uploading sensitive content.

### Coach, advisor, or facilitator

This is the strongest early paid buyer. They use the professional pack to guide
clients, prepare sessions, protect privacy, translate patterns into work
language, and keep interpretation non-diagnostic.

### Employer, manager, or HR buyer

The employer-side buyer pays when there is a concrete role-fit, retention,
contribution, or team-development problem. Their use must stay role/context
oriented. They should not buy the product to rank employees, validate talent,
select candidates, decide promotions, manage performance, or determine support
entitlement.

### Founder-led early buyer

Founder-led buyers are people or organizations willing to test the package with
direct guidance. They are valuable because they reveal what needs clearer
facilitation, privacy language, examples, and packaging before self-serve
payment.

### Who should not be targeted yet

Do not target:

- Employers seeking assessment, selection, promotion, performance, ranking, or
  high-potential tools.
- Buyers asking for proof of giftedness, IQ measurement, diagnosis, or
  certification.
- Automated HR analytics, talent dashboards, or people-scoring vendors.
- Legal, clinical, occupational-health, disability, or formal accommodation use
  cases as the primary buyer route.
- Large enterprises wanting standardized employee rollout before the tool has
  been tested with controlled pilots.
- Users in adversarial, disciplinary, unsafe, or legally sensitive contexts
  without appropriate specialist support.

## 5. Packaging logic

### What should remain free

- Product overview and one-liner.
- What the tool is and is not.
- Responsible-use boundary language.
- "When not to use this tool" guidance.
- A free mini-canvas, checklist, or sample 30-day experiment format.
- Sample pages that show the non-diagnostic workflow.
- FAQ for giftedness labels, privacy, employer misuse, burnout, accommodation,
  and unsafe contexts.
- Invite-only beta route now; future beta interest form only after privacy
  review, consent, retention, and deletion handling are ready.

Free material should create trust and prevent misuse. It should not collect
completed scans.

### What should be paid

- Complete individual workbook bundle.
- Printable/editable workbook formats when export quality is ready.
- Professional/facilitator pack.
- Facilitator scripts and session agendas.
- Synthetic worked examples.
- Full employer responsible-use materials.
- 30-day experiment tracker and review templates.
- Professional update access during the v1 cycle.
- Workshops, guided implementation, and pilot support.

### What should require facilitation

- Employer/team pilots.
- Sensitive adult-manager conversations.
- Sharp disagreement about evidence.
- Situations with possible conflict, exit risk, values friction, overdrive, or
  privacy risk.
- Any use where the employer has influence over HR outcomes.
- Any use where the adult may feel pressured to disclose personal information.

Facilitation does not make the tool clinical, legal, or occupational-health
advice. It simply helps keep the role-design conversation bounded.

### What should not be sold until tested

- Broad employer self-serve licenses.
- Certification programs.
- Train-the-trainer programs.
- Enterprise dashboards.
- Account-based completion, storage, or analytics for personal responses.
- Claims of validation, prediction, norming, or evidence-based assessment.
- Any automated interpretation, recommendation, scoring, or ranking.
- Public claims that the tool reduces churn, burnout, conflict, or performance
  issues unless evidence exists.

### What must not be packaged as diagnosis, assessment, ranking, hiring, or performance management

Never package the tool as:

- A giftedness diagnosis, IQ proxy, psychometric assessment, or talent test.
- A hiring, selection, promotion, performance-review, high-potential, or
  employee-ranking instrument.
- A burnout, mental-health, disability, occupational-health, or legal
  determination.
- A way to decide support entitlement.
- A dashboard that processes completed personal responses.
- A standard for comparing employees.

## 6. Conversion path

### Visitor to beta lead

Visitor sees the role-design promise, non-diagnostic boundaries, audience paths,
sample pages, and pricing hypotheses. They request beta access or a preview
because the language matches a real work-context problem.

Safe claims now:

- The tool is a structured workbook and facilitation package.
- It helps users prepare a role-design conversation.
- It aims to produce one bounded 30-day experiment.
- It is non-diagnostic and not for HR decisions.

Claims requiring evidence:

- It reliably improves retention, energy, performance, conflict outcomes, or
  manager behavior.
- It is validated, predictive, normed, clinically reviewed, or legally reviewed.

### Beta lead to structured user

Lead receives or is offered a controlled beta package, orientation, and
responsible-use note. They use the workbook privately, with a coach, or in a
facilitated session. Feedback focuses on clarity, safety, usefulness, concrete
experiments, and confusion risk.

### Structured user to paid individual

An adult who has used the preview or beta buys the workbook when they need a
complete private preparation flow, disclosure planning, and a 30-day experiment
template. This should remain optional; sensitive situations may need
facilitation instead.

### Coach/advisor to professional pack

A coach, HR partner, advisor, or facilitator buys when they see repeated client
or workplace situations and need reusable scripts, handouts, examples,
responsible-use guidance, and update access.

### Employer to team/workshop

An employer should convert through a scoped pilot, workshop, or guided
implementation, not immediate self-serve checkout. The conversion trigger is a
specific role-fit or contribution problem where responsible-use boundaries are
accepted in advance.

### Safe claims now and claims requiring evidence

| Claim type | Safe now | Requires evidence before using |
|---|---|---|
| Product form | Markdown-first workbook and facilitation package | Validated commercial instrument |
| Output | One bounded role-design experiment | Guaranteed improvement |
| Use case | Conversation preparation and role/context review | Performance, health, legal, or HR decision support |
| Buyer value | Saves preparation time and gives safer structure | Proven ROI, churn reduction, burnout prevention, or productivity gain |
| Interpretation | Qualitative hypotheses from work examples | Diagnosis, scoring, prediction, normed assessment, or ranking |

## 7. Payment implementation plan

This section is a plan only. Do not implement payments yet.

### Recommended provider

Use Stripe unless later repository context suggests another provider. Stripe is
a practical default because it can support:

- One-time product purchases.
- Subscriptions for professional update access.
- Hosted Checkout without building a custom payment backend.
- Download fulfillment through manual or third-party delivery at first.
- EU VAT and invoice workflows, if configured correctly.

Do not connect Stripe until pricing, terms, privacy, refund policy, delivery,
and support workflow are ready.

### Products and prices to create later

Likely Stripe products:

- Free preview: no paid product; invite-only beta route for now.
- Individual workbook: one-time product, likely EUR 39-79.
- Professional/facilitator pack: annual subscription or one-time early license,
  likely EUR 249-499 annual or EUR 299-699 early one-time.
- Employer pilot: manual invoice product, likely EUR 1,500-4,500.
- Workshop/guided implementation: manual invoice product, likely EUR 750-7,500
  depending on scope.
- Custom advisory/enterprise: quote-only manual invoice.

### What would be downloadable

- Individual workbook PDF/Markdown/DOCX package when export quality is verified.
- Free checklist or mini-canvas.
- Professional pack files: workbook, interpretation guide, manager guide,
  facilitator materials, handouts, examples, tracker, review template, and
  responsible-use checklist.
- Release notes and version history.

Downloads should be blank templates and guidance only, not completed scans.

### What would require account access later

Account access may be useful later for:

- Professional update access.
- License management.
- Download history.
- Organization pilot materials.
- Support requests.

Do not build account access until download delivery, support volume, update
policy, and privacy requirements justify it.

### Manual payment option for early beta

Manual payment is appropriate during early beta:

- Use invoice or bank transfer for professional packs, workshops, and employer
  pilots.
- Keep individual beta sales manual if privacy/terms are not ready for public
  checkout.
- Manually qualify employer requests before payment.
- Send download links only after responsible-use terms are accepted.

Manual payment is slower, but safer while the offer and boundaries are still
being tested.

### Refund and cancellation considerations

- Individual workbook: consider a simple 14-day refund window if no abuse is
  apparent.
- Professional pack: define whether refunds apply after download, and whether
  updates are included for a fixed period.
- Subscription option: allow cancellation before renewal; state whether access
  to previously downloaded materials remains.
- Workshops and pilots: define cancellation windows, rescheduling rules, and
  what preparation work is non-refundable.
- Custom advisory: use scoped statements of work.

Refund language should not imply outcomes are guaranteed.

### VAT and EU invoicing considerations

At a high level:

- Decide whether sales are B2C, B2B, or both.
- Configure VAT handling before selling in the EU.
- Collect billing details needed for invoices, but avoid collecting sensitive
  workbook or HR case content.
- Consider reverse-charge handling for EU B2B customers where applicable.
- Keep records for accounting and tax obligations.
- Get professional tax/accounting advice before public launch.

This plan is not legal, tax, accounting, or compliance advice.

### Security and privacy implications

- Do not collect completed workbook responses through payment, beta, support,
  or feedback forms.
- Keep lead forms minimal: name, email, role/audience type, country if needed
  for VAT, and consent.
- Use hosted checkout rather than storing card data.
- Publish privacy and terms pages before live beta collection or payment.
- Define retention and access rules for leads, customers, invoices, support
  messages, and workshop notes.
- Keep employer purchases separate from employee personal workbook content.
- Do not add analytics on personal responses.

## 9. Launch offer

### Private beta offer

Offer a small private beta for adults, coaches, HR partners, facilitators, and
managers who can respect the boundaries. The offer should test clarity, safety,
usefulness, terminology, workflow, and packaging.

Possible framing:

> Private beta for a workbook-first role-design tool that helps high-complexity
> adults and work contexts move from vague tension to one bounded 30-day
> experiment.

### Founder-led facilitation offer

Offer a limited number of founder-led sessions or guided implementations to
learn where the tool needs clearer instructions, examples, and safeguards.

Keep the scope narrow:

- One preparation call.
- One facilitated role-design session or workshop.
- One 30-day experiment plan.
- Optional review call.
- Written feedback from participants.

### Early professional pack

Offer the professional pack to a small number of coaches, HR partners, and
facilitators at an early price in exchange for structured feedback. Include a
clear license scope and no certification claim.

### Employer pilot

Offer employer pilots only when responsible-use boundaries are accepted:

- No hiring, selection, promotion, performance, ranking, support-entitlement,
  diagnosis, health, legal, or occupational-health use.
- No collection of completed personal scans by the product owner.
- Focus on role/context conditions and one bounded experiment.
- Facilitated onboarding and review.

### What to avoid promising

Avoid promising:

- Diagnosis, validation, or proof of giftedness.
- Improved performance, retention, engagement, or health outcomes.
- Reduced burnout, conflict, absenteeism, or churn.
- Legal, HR, occupational-health, disability, or compliance readiness.
- Certification for coaches or HR.
- A fully localized bilingual product unless it has been produced and reviewed.
- Software, accounts, dashboards, analytics, or automated interpretation.

## 10. Revenue milestones

### First 10 beta users

Goal: test whether the positioning attracts the right users and whether the
beta screen can exclude unsafe or unsuitable uses.

Evidence to collect:

- Audience type.
- Use context.
- What they expected the tool to solve.
- Whether the boundaries were understood.
- What would make them pay or not pay.

### First 3 paid facilitated uses

Goal: test whether founder-led sessions create useful experiments and reveal
where facilitation materials need strengthening.

Success signal:

- Each use ends in a concrete 30-day experiment or a responsible decision to
  pause/escalate.

### First 10 paying individual users

Goal: test whether adults will pay for a self-guided workbook without expecting
diagnosis, validation, or employer leverage.

Success signal:

- Buyers report that they prepared better, chose disclosure boundaries, and
  could name a small role-design request.

### First professional/facilitator buyer

Goal: confirm that the professional pack is credible as a paid tool.

Success signal:

- Buyer uses it with at least one real or realistic case and gives structured
  feedback.

### First employer pilot

Goal: test whether an employer will accept responsible-use constraints and pay
  for a scoped role-design pilot.

Success signal:

- Employer agrees to role/context focus, privacy limits, and no HR decision use.

### First EUR 1k revenue

Likely path:

- One small workshop or several professional/individual sales.

Learning goal:

- Identify which offer converts fastest without increasing misuse risk.

### First EUR 5k revenue

Likely path:

- One employer pilot plus professional pack sales, or multiple workshops.

Learning goal:

- Determine whether the product should scale through professional packs,
  employer pilots, workshops, or individual workbook sales.

## 12. Commercial learning plan

### What needs to be tested before building payments

- Which audience path converts to serious beta interest.
- Whether people understand the non-diagnostic boundary.
- Whether adults can use the workbook privately without oversharing.
- Whether professionals can facilitate from the materials.
- Whether managers can use role/context language without performance framing.
- Which package people believe is worth paying for.
- Which price ranges create commitment without overpromising.
- Which parts are too abstract, too sensitive, too long, or missing.
- Whether support questions can be handled with FAQ and guides.
- Whether privacy, terms, VAT, and delivery needs are clear enough for live
  payment.

### Interview questions

Ask beta users and potential buyers:

- What work situation made this tool feel relevant or irrelevant?
- Which phrase described the problem best?
- Which phrase created concern or confusion?
- What would you expect to receive in the free preview?
- What would make the individual workbook worth paying for?
- What would make the professional pack worth paying for?
- Would you use this alone, with a coach, with a manager, or only with
  facilitation?
- What would you refuse to share with an employer?
- What would make this unsafe or inappropriate in your context?
- What evidence after 30 days would make you say it helped?
- What would make you ask for a refund?
- What claims would you distrust?

### Beta success criteria

Private beta is successful when:

- At least 10 qualified beta users or evaluators complete structured feedback.
- At least 3 facilitated uses produce a concrete experiment or responsible
  pause/escalation decision.
- Most users can explain what the tool is not for.
- Adults report that disclosure choices were clearer.
- Professionals report that the flow is usable without author explanation.
- Managers or employers can name role levers and legitimate constraints.
- Misuse attempts are identified and can be mitigated in copy, guides, or terms.
- At least one buyer is willing to pay for a professional pack, workshop, or
  pilot.

### Price sensitivity questions

- At EUR 39, what would you expect in the individual workbook?
- At EUR 79, what would need to be included?
- Would you prefer one-time purchase, annual updates, or a guided session?
- For professionals, is EUR 249-499 credible for a reusable pack?
- What usage limit would feel fair for a professional beta license?
- For an employer pilot, what budget range is realistic for a scoped workshop
  and review?
- Which offer feels safest: individual workbook, professional pack, workshop,
  or employer pilot?
- What would make the price feel too high or too low?

### Signals to continue

Continue with monetization if:

- Beta leads are qualified and understand boundaries.
- Users reach concrete 30-day experiments.
- Professionals want reusable materials and are willing to pay.
- Employer buyers accept responsible-use constraints.
- Price conversations produce real willingness to pay, not only interest.
- Support and privacy concerns can be handled with planned guides and terms.

### Signals to pivot

Pivot if:

- Most leads want diagnosis, validation, certification, ranking, or HR decision
  support.
- Adults find the tool too sensitive to use without heavy facilitation.
- Professionals do not see enough reusable value.
- Employers only want assessment, standardization, or performance linkage.
- The language does not work outside a small giftedness-aware audience.
- The support burden requires custom consulting for nearly every use.
- Privacy or misuse risks cannot be reduced with current packaging.
