# High-Complexity Contribution Tool

Also named **Instrument Complexiteit naar Bijdrage**.

The High-Complexity Contribution Tool helps high-complexity adults, managers,
HR partners, and facilitators turn vague role-fit tension into a safe, concrete
30-day work experiment. It is practical, employer-friendly, and focused on
sustainable contribution without diagnosis, scoring, or HR ranking.

This is not an IQ test, giftedness diagnosis, hiring tool, promotion tool,
performance rating, high-potential ranking, clinical assessment, burnout
diagnosis, occupational-health instrument, or disability determination.

## What This Tool Is

This repository contains a Markdown-first workbook prototype for shared
conversation and role design. It gives adults and work environments a structured
way to discuss where cognitive complexity, learning speed, autonomy needs,
quality sensitivity, intensity, sparring needs, translation work, energy, and
boundaries are either converted into useful contribution or blocked by the role
context.

The tool is built around person-role-context fit. The intended output is not a
label or score. The intended output is a bounded role-design hypothesis and one
30-day work experiment that can be reviewed with concrete evidence.

## Who It Is For

Primary users:

- Coaches, career counselors, HR partners, independent facilitators, and
  people-development professionals who work with gifted, hoogbegaafde,
  neurodivergent, fast-learning, or high-complexity adults.

Secondary users:

- Gifted, hoogbegaafde, or high-complexity adults who feel underused,
  overloaded, misunderstood, constrained, or near conflict or exit in work.
- Managers and employers who want to improve role conditions without turning
  the conversation into diagnosis, special pleading, performance judgment, or
  talent ranking.
- HR teams that need careful language for role design, contribution, learning,
  autonomy, quality dialogue, sparring, translation, energy, and boundaries.

Relevant contexts:

- Coaching and career guidance.
- Manager-employee role-fit conversations.
- HR or people-development conversations about sustainable contribution.
- Facilitated work-context conversations where complexity, autonomy, learning,
  values, intensity, or energy are part of the pattern.

Excluded uses:

- Proving giftedness or measuring IQ.
- Hiring, selection, promotion, performance review, high-potential ranking, or
  deciding whether someone deserves support.
- Clinical, legal, occupational-health, disability, burnout, or formal
  accommodation decisions.
- Unsafe, adversarial, disciplinary, or legally sensitive situations without
  appropriate specialist support.
- Automated analytics, dashboards, ranking, or interpretation of completed
  personal responses.

## Problem Addressed

High cognitive complexity is often not converted into contribution. In work
settings it can be missed, pathologized, reduced to a giftedness debate, or
misread as attitude, resistance, fragility, arrogance, boredom, conflict, or
inconsistency.

The tool helps make these patterns discussable without turning them into a
diagnosis:

- Complexity fit: whether the work is intellectually complex, systemic,
  ambiguous, and non-routine enough.
- Autonomy fit: whether there is enough freedom in method, sequencing, framing,
  and decision-making within legitimate constraints.
- Learning fit: whether the role still offers novelty, mastery, growth, and a
  visible next learning edge.
- Values and quality fit: whether logic, fairness, ethics, integrity, risk, and
  meaningful standards can be discussed seriously.
- Sparring and translation fit: whether complex thinking can be challenged and
  translated into usable decisions or next steps.
- Energy and boundary fit: whether cognitive, emotional, social, sensory,
  meeting, focus, and recovery load are sustainable.

Employer-side confusion is treated as part of the role context. The tool helps
managers distinguish legitimate constraints from avoidable process noise, and
turn broad tensions around performance, friction, intensity, boredom, trust,
autonomy, or role design into a smaller testable change.

## Core Workflow

1. The adult completes or partially completes the Adult Self-Scan privately.
2. The adult chooses concrete examples and decides what to keep private, what
   may be shared, and what requires support before sharing.
3. The manager, employer, HR partner, or facilitator completes the Employer Role
   Scan from the role and context side.
4. Both sides bring only safe, useful material into the Joint Conversation
   Canvas.
5. The conversation identifies what is working, where capacity may be hidden or
   blocked, and which fit dimension matters most now.
6. The group chooses one working pathway hypothesis, such as hidden potential,
   underchallenge, overdrive risk, conflict or exit risk, or sustainable
   contribution.
7. The group designs one 30-day work experiment with adult actions, manager
   actions, protected workload, legitimate constraints, evidence, stop
   conditions, and a review date.
8. After 30 days, the group reviews evidence and chooses whether to continue,
   adjust, formalize, escalate, or stop.

## Commercial Direction

The likely commercial route is workbook-first rather than software-first.

The PRD recommends a website-first entry point that sells the job to be done:
turn complex capacity into sustainable contribution through a structured
role-design conversation. The website should include audience paths for adults,
managers, coaches/facilitators, and HR; visible responsible-use boundaries; a
three-part workflow overview; sample pages; and a simple free starter offer.

Early monetization hypothesis:

- Free lead magnet: conversation checklist, mini-canvas, sample pages, and
  responsible-use note.
- Paid individual workbook: adult scan, joint canvas, disclosure guide, and
  30-day tracker.
- Professional pack or license: full facilitator-ready toolkit for coaches, HR
  partners, and people-development professionals.
- Organization pilot or services layer: facilitated sessions, manager training,
  workshops, implementation support, or responsible-use onboarding.

The commercial model should avoid per-employee assessment pricing, giftedness
certification, automated ranking, and claims that the tool measures talent,
health, or performance.

## Safety, Ethics, and Positioning Boundaries

Use this tool only as a structured conversation and role-design aid.

Keep these boundaries visible:

- Non-diagnostic: it does not diagnose giftedness, neurodivergence, burnout,
  mental health, disability, or any clinical or occupational-health condition.
- Non-selective: it is not for hiring, selection, promotion, high-potential
  ranking, performance review, support entitlement, or employee ranking.
- Non-automated: it should not process completed personal responses into
  automated recommendations or dashboards.
- Privacy-first: completed scans may contain sensitive personal, workplace,
  health-adjacent, conflict, or HR information.
- Adult agency: the adult controls what personal information is kept private or
  shared.
- Employer responsibility: employer-side use must stay focused on role and
  context conditions, not ranking or diagnosing the person.
- Human review: interpretation must remain contextual, concrete, and reviewed by
  the people involved, ideally with facilitation when the situation is
  sensitive.
