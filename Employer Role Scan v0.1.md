# Employer Role Scan v0.1

## Purpose

This role scan helps a manager, employer, HR partner, or coach examine whether a role and work context can convert high-complexity capacity into sustainable contribution.

It asks:

> What does this role and context currently offer in terms of complexity, autonomy, learning, quality dialogue, sparring, translation, and sustainable load?

It does not ask:

> How gifted is this employee?

## Boundaries

This is not a selection, performance, promotion, high-potential, burnout, personality, IQ, or giftedness assessment. It should not be used to rank people, diagnose people, justify exclusion, or decide whether someone deserves support.

Low signals are not proof that a manager is bad. They indicate that the current role or context may be closing contribution conditions.

## How to Use

For each prompt, mark the current signal and add one concrete example from the role context.

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Strong fit | The role/context currently supports contribution. |
| Workable but fragile | Fit exists but may fail under pressure. |
| Fit gap | A mismatch is likely blocking contribution. |
| Risk signal | Misfit may feed hidden potential, bore-out, overdrive, conflict, or exit. |
| Experiment priority | This is a good candidate for a 30-day role experiment. |
| Not sure | More evidence or conversation is needed. |

## 1. Complexity Fit / Complexiteitsfit

Core question:

> Does the role contain enough intellectual complexity, systemic thinking, ambiguity, and non-routine problem-solving?

Prompts:

1. The role contains real problems to analyze, not only tasks to execute.
2. The employee can work on ambiguous, systemic, cross-functional, or non-routine issues.
3. The role can absorb high-quality ideas without requiring the person to create an unofficial second job.
4. Routine work is balanced with complex contribution opportunities.
5. The person can sometimes challenge the problem frame, not only deliver within it.
6. Complex work is allocated deliberately rather than only through informal escalation.

Role lever to consider:

> Add one bounded complexity lane, such as problem analysis, options analysis, process improvement, risk review, or system mapping.

## 2. Autonomy Fit / Autonomiefit

Core question:

> Does the role provide enough freedom in method, sequencing, judgment, and problem framing within legitimate boundaries?

Prompts:

1. Outcomes and constraints are clear enough.
2. Method freedom exists where standardization is not genuinely necessary.
3. Decision rights are explicit.
4. The person can influence relevant choices before decisions are fully fixed.
5. Approval loops are proportionate to risk.
6. Necessary rules, procedures, and safety constraints are explained clearly.

Role lever to consider:

> Define three zones: here you decide, here we align, here the organization decides.

## 3. Learning Fit / Leerfit

Core question:

> Does the role continue to offer growth, novelty, mastery, and development after the initial learning curve?

Prompts:

1. The role has a visible next learning edge.
2. The person can access new domains, stakeholders, methods, or levels of responsibility.
3. Fast mastery does not trap the person in repetitive maintenance.
4. Development is built into the work, not only into courses.
5. There is room for stretch assignments with bounded workload.
6. The role phase fits the person sufficiently: discovery, design, analysis, implementation, maintenance, or troubleshooting.

Role lever to consider:

> Create a 30-day learning sprint with a clear question, scope, and review moment.

## 4. Values & Quality Fit / Waarden- en Kwaliteitsfit

Core question:

> Can quality, logic, fairness, ethics, integrity, and meaningful standards be discussed seriously?

Prompts:

1. Quality and risk concerns have a credible discussion route.
2. Trade-offs between speed, cost, risk, and quality are made explicit.
3. Critical thinking is treated as potential contribution, not automatically as resistance.
4. The manager or team can distinguish principle, risk, preference, timing, and perfectionism.
5. "Good enough" standards are explained rather than imposed as vague lowering of quality.
6. Ethical, safety, fairness, or integrity issues can be escalated without avoidable personal conflict.

Role lever to consider:

> Use a structured quality dialogue: issue, evidence, risk, trade-off, decision, owner, review moment.

## 5. Sparring & Translation Fit / Sparring- en Vertaalfit

Core question:

> Is there enough intellectual sparring, and can complex thinking be translated into usable organizational language?

Prompts:

1. The person has access to adequate intellectual sparring.
2. Complex ideas can be tested before high-stakes decisions.
3. The manager helps convert complex insights into usable decisions, options, or next steps.
4. The person receives feedback on clarity, timing, and stakeholder impact.
5. Fast pattern recognition is not automatically interpreted as arrogance, resistance, or overcomplication.
6. Recognition is given for reasoning and expertise without turning it into status inflation.

Role lever to consider:

> Use a one-page translation format: observation, implication, recommendation, risk of inaction.

## 6. Energy & Boundary Fit / Energie- en Grenzenfit

Core question:

> Is the role sustainable in cognitive load, emotional load, sensory load, meeting load, focus time, recovery, and boundaries?

Prompts:

1. Meeting load leaves enough focus time for complex work.
2. Workload and priorities are explicit enough to prevent constant over-responsibility.
3. The person is not used as an informal problem-solver without role clarity or recognition.
4. Cognitive, emotional, social, and sensory load are visible enough to discuss.
5. High standards are not allowed to become chronic overdrive.
6. Recovery after intense work periods is possible.

Role lever to consider:

> Run a meeting and focus-time review, then protect specific blocks for complex work.

## Leadership and Context Conditions / Leiderschap en Contextvoorwaarden

Leadership is treated here as a context shaper, not as a separate diagnostic category and not as the whole model.

Prompts:

1. I clarify outcomes and constraints while allowing freedom in method where possible.
2. I can explain which rules or procedures are genuinely non-negotiable.
3. I invite substantive challenge before decisions become fixed.
4. I treat critical thinking as potential contribution, not automatically as resistance.
5. I help this person translate complex insights into usable decisions or next steps.
6. I notice whether the role is underusing, overloading, or misreading this person.
7. I provide or arrange adequate sparring when the direct line is too narrow.
8. I distinguish avoidable process noise from necessary structure.

Interpretation:

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Low clarity + low method freedom | Autonomy fit gap may be closing contribution. |
| Low challenge invitation + low quality dialogue | Values/quality fit may be moving toward conflict risk. |
| Low sparring + low recognition | Hidden potential may be present. |
| High structure + low explanation + low influence | Hierarchical friction may be a context marker. |
| High responsibility + low focus/recovery | Overdrive risk may be rising. |

## Pathway Hypothesis

Use this as a working hypothesis only.

| Role/context pattern | Possible pathway |
|---|---|
| Low complexity + low learning + low energy | Bore-out / underchallenge |
| High capacity + low recognition + low sparring | Hidden potential |
| High responsibility + high standards + weak recovery | Overdrive / burnout risk |
| Low autonomy + low values/quality safety + repeated misunderstanding | Conflict / exit risk |
| Sufficient complexity + autonomy + learning + sparring + sustainable energy | Sustainable contribution |
| Low autonomy + high structure + low explanation + low influence | Hierarchical friction as context marker |
