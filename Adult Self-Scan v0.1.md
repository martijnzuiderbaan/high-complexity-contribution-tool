## Purpose

This self-scan helps a gifted, hoogbegaafde, or high-complexity adult prepare a grounded conversation about sustainable contribution at work.

It does not answer:

> Am I officially gifted?

It helps answer:

> Where is my high cognitive complexity, learning speed, autonomy need, values sensitivity, intensity, and energy pattern being converted into useful contribution, and where is it blocked, hidden, overused, or misunderstood?

## Boundaries

This is a structured reflection tool, not a psychometric test. Do not use it for diagnosis, IQ measurement, hiring, selection, promotion, performance review, burnout diagnosis, occupational-health decisions, or disability determination.

The adult decides what to keep private and what to share.

## How to Use

For each prompt, mark the current signal and add one concrete example.

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Strong fit | This condition currently supports contribution. |
| Workable but fragile | Fit exists but may fail under pressure. |
| Fit gap | A mismatch is likely blocking contribution. |
| Risk signal | Misfit may feed hidden potential, bore-out, overdrive, conflict, or exit. |
| Experiment priority | This is a good candidate for a 30-day work experiment. |
| Not sure | More reflection or evidence is needed. |

## 1. Complexity Fit / Complexiteitsfit

Core question:

> Is the work intellectually complex, systemic, ambiguous, non-routine, and cognitively engaging enough?

Prompts:

1. My work regularly asks me to connect multiple variables, people, systems, risks, or time horizons.
2. I deal with enough ambiguous, novel, or non-routine problems.
3. My role uses my thinking capacity without requiring constant overdrive.
4. I can question the framing of a problem, not only answer within a fixed frame.
5. Routine work is balanced by enough analysis, design, problem exploration, or problem-solving.
6. My strongest insights are noticed and used rather than treated as side comments.
7. The role has a next level of complexity available after I master the current work.
8. I can see how my complex thinking creates useful value for the organization, client, team, or mission.

Reflection:

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| Where does complexity currently support my contribution? |  |
| Where am I underused or mentally idle? |  |
| Where do I create unnecessary complexity myself? |  |
| What would be one practical way to add better complexity? |  |

## 2. Autonomy Fit / Autonomiefit

Core question:

> Is there enough freedom in method, sequencing, problem framing, and decision-making within legitimate boundaries?

Prompts:

1. The desired outcome is clear enough, while the method is not over-controlled.
2. I have enough freedom to sequence my work in a way that fits the problem.
3. My decision rights are clear.
4. I can influence problem framing before decisions are fully fixed.
5. Constraints are explained as necessary boundaries rather than arbitrary control.
6. I am not micromanaged when I have demonstrated competence.
7. Autonomy does not become abandonment; I can get alignment when needed.
8. I know which decisions are mine, which require alignment, and which belong to the organization.

Reflection:

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| Where do I have useful autonomy now? |  |
| Where is autonomy too narrow, unclear, or unsafe? |  |
| Where do I need to understand organizational constraints better? |  |
| What would clearer outcome boundaries plus method freedom look like? |  |

## 3. Learning Fit / Leerfit

Core question:

> Does the role offer continuing growth, novelty, mastery, and development after the initial learning curve?

Prompts:

1. I still have meaningful things to learn in this role.
2. The role gives me access to new domains, methods, stakeholders, or levels of responsibility.
3. I am not stuck in maintenance or repetition after quickly mastering the basics.
4. I can deepen mastery rather than only increase volume.
5. The work includes discovery, design, analysis, or troubleshooting where possible.
6. I get enough feedback to sharpen my judgment.
7. Learning opportunities are not only formal training; the work itself develops me.
8. The next learning edge is visible and realistic.

Reflection:

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| What am I still learning here? |  |
| Where has the learning curve flattened too much? |  |
| Which role phase fits me best right now: discovery, design, analysis, implementation, maintenance, or troubleshooting? |  |
| What would a 30-day learning sprint test? |  |

## 4. Values & Quality Fit / Waarden- en Kwaliteitsfit

Core question:

> Can quality, logic, fairness, ethics, integrity, and meaningful standards be discussed seriously?

Prompts:

1. I can raise quality concerns without being treated as difficult by default.
2. Ethical, safety, fairness, or integrity issues have a credible route for discussion.
3. Trade-offs between speed, cost, risk, and quality are discussed honestly.
4. I can distinguish a genuine quality issue from preference, timing, or perfectionism.
5. I am not expected to silently lower standards in ways that create avoidable risk or meaninglessness.
6. The organization can explain when "good enough" is appropriate.
7. My values sensitivity helps improve work rather than only creating friction.
8. The work connects enough to a meaningful purpose, client value, craft standard, or mission.

Reflection:

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| Which quality or values issues are real and important? |  |
| Which issues may be preference, perfectionism, or poor timing? |  |
| What would a better route for quality dialogue look like? |  |
| What am I willing to accept as a legitimate trade-off? |  |

## 5. Sparring & Translation Fit / Sparring- en Vertaalfit

Core question:

> Is there enough intellectual sparring, and can complex thinking be translated into language others can use?

Prompts:

1. I have access to people who can challenge my thinking at enough depth.
2. I can test ideas before they become high-stakes or politically sensitive.
3. I can translate complex insights into usable decisions, options, or next steps.
4. I receive feedback on whether my message lands.
5. My thinking speed or pattern recognition is not automatically interpreted as arrogance, resistance, or overcomplication.
6. I can slow down enough to bring others along when needed.
7. The role gives me enough recognition for reasoning and expertise without requiring status inflation.
8. I am not isolated as the informal person who sees problems but has no route to make them useful.

Reflection:

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| Who gives me useful sparring now? |  |
| Where do I need better translation, timing, or framing? |  |
| Where am I being misunderstood? |  |
| What one-page format could make my insight more usable? |  |

## 6. Energy & Boundary Fit / Energie- en Grenzenfit

Core question:

> Is the work sustainable in cognitive load, emotional load, sensory load, meeting load, focus time, recovery, perfectionism, and boundaries?

Prompts:

1. The work uses energy without chronically draining recovery.
2. Meeting load leaves enough focus time for complex work.
3. Cognitive, emotional, sensory, and social load are manageable.
4. I can set boundaries without feeling I am failing the work or others.
5. I am not repeatedly pulled into rescue work without role clarity or recognition.
6. My standards do not push me into constant over-responsibility.
7. I can recover after intense work periods.
8. The role does not rely on me compensating for structural problems indefinitely.

Reflection:

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| Which parts of the work energize me? |  |
| Which load factors are unsustainable? |  |
| Where am I overusing strength, responsibility, or speed? |  |
| What boundary or meeting/focus change would be worth testing? |  |
